# 02 — Async Delivery Framework

This document is the **operational manual** for running Expand the Box as a self-paced asynchronous online course. It is the document a community manager, course operator, or solo cohort steward should read in full before opening a cohort.

The course is designed to work without a live facilitator on every module. It is **not** designed to work without structure. The structure is here.

---

## A. The learner's week

A learner moves through one module per **~3 days** (faster or slower depending on life). The recommended rhythm for a single module:

| Day | Action | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Watch the module video(s) + read the module write-up | 60–90 min |
| Day 1 | Solo embodied practice | 10–20 min |
| Day 1 or 2 | Record partner check-in (voice message) | 5–10 min |
| Day 2–3 | Run the between-module experiment in real life | passive — happens during ordinary day |
| Day 3 | Listen to partner's reply; record reflection back | 10–15 min |
| Day 3 | Write journal reflections; post one line to cohort feed | 15 min |

Total active time per module: **~2 hours on Low-intensity days, ~3–3.5 hours on High-intensity days (5, 6, 7, 9)** — see each day's header for its own estimate; the per-module figures are authoritative, this is the range. The between-module experiment adds zero scheduled time — it is the rep the learner runs *during* their already-scheduled life (a hard conversation, a meeting, dinner with a partner). The async partner-reply latency (up to a 24h SLA) can extend the calendar time of High modules beyond the active hours, especially Day 6.

At ~3 days per module × 10 modules = **~30 days** end to end. Faster cohorts can compress to 20 days; slower cohorts can stretch to 60. The architecture does not care, as long as the rhythm of *content → practice → experiment → reflection* is preserved.

## B. Module artifacts (what the learner actually receives)

Each module delivers, in order:

1. **A short orientation video** (5–10 min) — frame for the module, recorded by a PM trainer or course host. Recommends pace, names intensity tag, points to safety callouts.
2. **The relevant concept lecture(s)** from the source library — one or two of the 19 numbered videos, plus any short mini-lectures (e.g., "What Is Your Box," "The Numbness Bar"). Linked, not re-uploaded, where licensing permits; otherwise mirrored to the course platform with attribution.
3. **The written module file** (`Day NN - …`.md) — the script-grade write-up of the module. The single canonical source for the day.
4. **Map Notes** for the maps introduced today.
5. **The partner prompt** — a one-page handout the learner sends to or shares with their pairing partner.
6. **The experiment card** — a single page with the between-module experiment, framed as a small specific rep.
7. **Reflection journal pages** — printable prompts.

All of the above ships as text or PDF the learner can download. Videos are streamed.

## C. Pairing partners

### Pairing as one strand of the training matrix

In PM, a **matrix** is a structured field that holds learning. The Expand the Box matrix is woven from several strands: the learner's declared context (the red-pill commitment), the four working agreements, the body practices (center · ground · bubble · golden cube), the Map Notes the learner studies, the cohort boundary, the CM's gatekeeping, and — one strand among these — **the partner pairing**.

The pairing is not the whole matrix. It is the witness strand. What the partner holds: a structured place to speak the learner's actual material out loud, a witness who listens without advising, a buddy whose presence interrupts isolation on the High modules. What the partner does **not** hold: the learner's safety container, the learner's clinical care, the learner's commitment to the work, or the learner's responsibility for their own state.

This is why the partner agreement (`04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` Section B) is signed before any exchange, why the pairing tracker exists (Doc 04 Section C), and why a learner without a live pairing cannot unlock High modules. The partner strand is not optional — but neither does it carry weight the other strands are meant to carry.

### Why pairing is structural

Pairing is structural, not optional. The course's relational learning (especially Days 5, 6, 7, 8, 9) depends on a learner having one specific other person to:

- Voice-message before high-intensity modules (so they are accompanied, not alone)
- Voice-message after, to be witnessed
- Practice listening, speaking, completion loops and problem ownership with — using their actual material, not role plays

### C.1 Matching rules

- **Match before Day 1.** Learners cannot start Module 1 without an assigned partner. If a learner enrolls late, match them before content access opens.
- **Match on cadence first, content preference second.** Two learners who can both message daily but live in incompatible time zones will struggle less than two perfectly philosophically matched learners who can only meet weekly.
- **Avoid pre-existing close ties.** Spouses, business partners, very close friends bring entanglement that compromises the practice. If pre-existing ties are unavoidable in a small cohort, name the dynamic explicitly and add a third "witness partner."
- **Re-match by Day 4 if it isn't working.** If the partnership has not produced one real exchange by Day 4, the community manager re-matches. Silent partnerships are worse than swapped ones.

### C.2 Voice-message exchanges (the default mode)

Voice messages are preferred to text. They carry tone, breath, and the texture of the learner's emotional body — text strips this. Recommended platforms: Telegram, WhatsApp, Voxer, or Signal. The course does not impose one, **but the platform must meet the privacy minimums in `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` Section H** — private 1:1 thread, no automatic AI transcription, no public forwarding, deletion option, and (preferably) end-to-end encryption. Public Discord servers, group chats, and platforms that auto-summarize voice notes do not qualify.

**Default exchange shape per module:**
- Learner A records 3–8 min responding to the module's partner prompt
- Learner B listens within 24 hours, then records 3–8 min back: *what I heard · what I noticed in myself · one question I'm leaving with you*
- Optionally: a second round if either partner wants it

**What partners do NOT do:**
- Advise
- Fix
- Diagnose
- "Hold space" in a generic way
- Pretend to feel something they do not feel

What they do is **listen, witness, and reflect back what they actually heard**. The course teaches this on Day 8 (4 Listenings + Speakings). Until then, learners have an explicit "we're learning this" frame for any clumsy exchange.

### C.3 Scheduled async calls (alternative)

For partners who prefer real-time voice over recorded voice messages, a 30-minute scheduled call substitutes for the exchange. Same structure: each partner gets ~10 min uninterrupted, with the other listening.

## D. The cohort feed

A single shared space — forum thread, Slack/Discord channel, group voice thread, or shared doc — where learners post brief reflections after each module.

**What learners post:**
- One line of what landed
- One line of what is sticking sideways
- (Optional) a question for the group
- (Optional) a witness offer: "if anyone needs a third party to listen to something this week, I have 20 min Thursday"

**What learners do NOT post:**
- Long therapeutic confessionals (the partner exchange is the place for that)
- Other people's stories
- Advice to peers (community manager redirects)

**Cohort feed moderation:**
- Community manager reads every post within 48 hours
- Every post is classified into one of three flags using the green/yellow/red **triage rubric** in `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` Section F. Yellow = DM within 24 hours. Red = immediate response (not within 24 hours), with crisis line numbers in the first message.
- Keeps the feed *narrow* — broad debate about PM theory belongs in a different forum

## E. Optional live integration calls

The course is async-first. Two **optional live calls** are recommended for any cohort:

1. **Mid-course integration call** — **after Day 6 (not after Day 5).** ~75 min. Facilitator-led. Placed here on purpose: Days 5, 6 and 7 are three High-intensity modules in a row, and Day 6 (the EHP) is the single heaviest. The call lands *after the learner is through the worst of the cliff and before Day 7*, so it functions as integration and a breath, not a warm-up. Format: short check-in round, one or two volunteer "live demos" of the new map of feelings / a recent EHP-map practice on a real situation, Q&A.
2. **End-of-course integration call** — after Day 10. ~90 min. Facilitator-led. Format: declarations round (each learner declares one thing they are taking forward), gratitude round to partners, what's-next pointers (further training, communities, books).

### The integration pause (built into the cadence, not optional)

**Insert a deliberate rest beat between Day 6 and Day 7.** This is the only place in the course where three High modules stack. After Day 6's EHP practice the learner is often tender for hours; opening Day 7 (Low Drama, the Gremlin) on top of that is how cohorts burn out or silently drop. The CM holds the cadence here: at least **one full low-demand day** between Day 6 and Day 7 — no new content, just ground, sleep, journal, and a partner check-in. Day 7's header repeats this. Learners on the compressed cadence (Doc Section I) still take this pause; it is not a module they can skip past.

**Recording rules.** Personal check-ins, demos, and shares are **never recorded.** Recording is permitted only for the teaching/Q&A segments and only if every participant in the call gives explicit consent at the start of that specific call. Consent given for one call does not carry forward. For absent learners, the facilitator provides a written summary of the teaching content (not the shares). See `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` Section H.

Live calls are *integration* — they don't introduce new content. A learner who skips them does not miss material.

## F. The community manager role

A community manager (CM) — often the cohort facilitator, but not necessarily a PM trainer — is required to operate a cohort safely. The CM is not the teacher; the modules are. The CM is the **container caretaker**.

### CM responsibilities

- **Pre-cohort:** matches partners; sends welcome packet; confirms each learner has the referral list (Section H) and knows how to reach the CM
- **Per-module:** monitors cohort feed; checks partner exchanges are happening (not their content — just that they exist); replies to direct messages within 24 hours
- **High-intensity weeks (Days 5–7):** lightweight check-in DM to every learner ("how's it landing? anything to flag?"); does not require a reply
- **Closure:** reminds learners how to access materials post-cohort; offers continued partner-pairing if both want it
- **Always:** holds the referral list; refers any learner who is over their head to professional support; does not provide therapy or coaching themselves

### CM skill floor

The CM is not necessarily a certified PM trainer or the teaching trainer for the cohort. But the role is non-trivial — see `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` Section J for the binding qualification bar: minimum completed Expand the Box plus one further PM training, current mental health first aid certification, a named clinical supervisor for case consultation, a written CM agreement with the operator, and professional liability insurance appropriate for the jurisdiction.

The skill floor (necessary but not sufficient) is the ability to:
- Hold confidentiality cleanly
- Distinguish emotional intensity (in scope) from crisis (out of scope)
- Refer without flinching
- Not collapse into rescuer mode when learners are uncomfortable
- Apply the triage rubric and the crisis escalation flow in Doc 04 Sections F and G without freezing

The CM's job is to keep the container intact. It is not to ensure no one feels uncomfortable. PM work involves discomfort. The container's job is to make discomfort survivable, not absent.

## G. Platform requirements

The course does not impose a platform. It needs:

- **Video hosting** (any platform that supports streaming and download for the concept videos)
- **Document hosting** (the `.md` files and PDFs of module write-ups, Map Notes, experiment cards, journal prompts)
- **Voice-message platform** (partner choice)
- **Cohort feed** (a forum, Slack/Discord, group thread)
- **A way for the CM to message learners 1:1**

Total: any combination of (Teachable / Podia / Kajabi / Notion / Circle) + (Telegram / WhatsApp / Voxer) + (Slack / Discord) works.

## H. The referral list

Every learner receives, at enrollment, a **per-learner referral packet** generated from the country, state/province, and language they declared at intake (see `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` Section A.1). The packet contains:

1. **One crisis line for the learner's country** — listed first, not buried
2. **Licensed mental health professionals** in the learner's region — directory link + any known PM-friendly clinicians
3. **PM-aware coaches** in the learner's language, with rates
4. **Local domestic violence and legal resources** if the coercive-environment screen returned yes
5. **A short not-therapy disclaimer**

The packet is generated and acknowledged **before** content access opens. The CM verifies. A generic facility-template version lives at `Facilitator Resources/Referral List.md`; the per-learner packet is the operational artifact.

## I. Cadence variants

| Variant | Pace | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Default** | ~3 days per module, ~30 days total | Best for most learners |
| **Compressed** | ~2 days per module, ~20 days total | Only for learners with bandwidth; risks shallow integration |
| **Stretched** | 4–6 days per module, 40–60 days total | Good for learners with demanding lives; works as long as the rhythm holds |
| **Pulsed** | Modules 1–2 in week 1, break 1 week, modules 3–4 in week 3, etc. | Good if life requires it; community manager makes sure partner pairings are still alive at re-entry |

Pace decisions are the learner's. The CM informs the partner so cadences match.

---

## J. What this framework deliberately leaves out

- **Gamification, badges, streaks, completion certificates.** They feed Gremlin's appetite for approval, comparison, and completion points. PM does not reward compliance.
- **Discussion forums for "questions about PM."** They become content consumption. The course's intellectual content is fixed; debate it elsewhere.
- **Replay-able quizzes.** PM is verified in lived experience, not multiple choice.
- **An app.** A platform is enough.

The framework is deliberately spare. The course's force is in the modules and the partner pairings, not in the infrastructure around them.
