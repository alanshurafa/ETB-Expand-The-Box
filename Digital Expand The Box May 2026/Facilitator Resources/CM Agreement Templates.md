# CM Agreement Templates

| | |
|---|---|

> **These are templates, not legal advice, and they are not legally sufficient as written.** They capture the operational obligations the safety framework requires (Doc 03, Doc 04) so nothing structural is forgotten. They are a starting point for a lawyer, not a substitute for one. **Have both agreements reviewed by a qualified attorney in the operator's and the CM's jurisdiction before anyone signs.** Employment vs. contractor status, liability allocation, insurance adequacy, data-protection obligations (GDPR, HIPAA-adjacent rules, local equivalents), and the enforceability of any clause vary by jurisdiction and by the parties' actual relationship. ## Template 1 — CM–Operator Agreement

**Community Manager Services Agreement**

This Agreement is entered into as of `[date]` between:

- **`[Operator legal name]`** ("the Operator"), the entity operating the Expand the Box async online course, of `[Operator address]`; and
- **`[CM legal name]`** ("the Community Manager" or "CM"), of `[CM address]`.

The Operator and the CM agree as follows.

### 1. Purpose and role

1.1 The Operator runs an asynchronous online course, *Expand the Box*, teaching the thoughtware of Possibility Management. The CM is engaged to serve as the **container caretaker** for one or more cohorts, as defined in `02 - Async Delivery Framework.md` §F and `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md`.

1.2 **The CM is not the teacher.** The course content (videos and written modules) does the teaching. The CM operates the safety container around it.

1.3 **The CM is not a therapist, counselor, clinician, or psychiatric provider, and is not engaged to provide therapy, counseling, trauma treatment, medical advice, psychiatric care, or coaching to any learner.** The course itself is not therapy or any of the foregoing (`03 - Safety and Facilitation Framework.md` §A). The CM's role is to keep the container intact, triage, hand off to qualified professionals, and document — not to treat (`04` §G, `03` §M).

### 2. Term and engagement

2.1 This Agreement covers the cohort(s) beginning on or about `[cohort start date(s)]` and continues until the post-cohort review for the final covered cohort is delivered (Doc 04 §L), unless terminated earlier under Section 12.

2.2 The CM is engaged as `[an independent contractor / an employee — select one; this choice has tax, liability, and insurance consequences and must be confirmed with counsel]`.

2.3 Compensation: `[fee / salary / per-cohort rate]`, payable `[schedule]`. `[Expense and reimbursement terms, if any.]`

### 3. Scope of the CM's duties

The CM will perform the duties set out in Doc 02 §F, Doc 03, and Doc 04, including without limitation:

3.1 **Pre-cohort:** apply the full intake screening protocol (Doc 04 §A — the five screens with their thresholds and actions); generate and verify a per-learner referral packet for each learner (Doc 04 §E); match partners (Doc 04 §A.1, §I; `Async Partner Pairing Playbook.md`); obtain signed partner agreements (Doc 04 §B); confirm the platform stack meets the privacy minimums (Doc 04 §H); and complete the pre-cohort checklist sign-off (Doc 04 §K) before content access opens.

3.2 **Per-module:** read every cohort-feed post within 48 hours (Doc 03 §K); classify every post and DM under the triage rubric (Doc 04 §F); maintain the pairing tracker and act on its mechanical triggers (Doc 04 §C); respond to routine learner direct messages within 24 hours (Doc 03 §G).

3.3 **High-intensity modules (Days 5, 6, 7, 9):** verify the unlock checklist per learner (Doc 04 §D); send the lightweight high-week check-in; run the mid-course pause prompt (Doc 02 §F; `Cohort Feed Templates.md` §6).

3.4 **Crisis response:** respond to any red-flag situation immediately on detection, with a first response within 15 minutes of the CM or the CM's named backup seeing the message, crisis-line numbers in that first reply, following the crisis escalation flow (Doc 04 §G). The CM does not provide clinical care; the CM stays present, hands off, and documents.

3.5 **Backup coverage:** identify and maintain a named backup CM who has accepted the role, holds platform access, and holds the crisis protocol, to cover any window in which the CM is unreachable, so the 15-minute red-flag response standard is met at all times (Doc 03 §G; `CM First-Cohort Run-Book.md` Part 5). `[Named backup CM: ___________. The backup's own engagement/coverage terms are set out in [a coverage clause here / a separate agreement].]`

3.6 **Closure and review:** close the cohort (Doc 02 §F; Playbook §8); conduct the post-cohort review within 1–2 weeks of Day 10 and share it with the Operator and the CM's clinical supervisor (Doc 04 §L).

### 4. CM qualifications and warranties

The CM represents and warrants that, for the duration of this Agreement, the CM meets the qualification bar in Doc 04 §J:

4.1 The CM has completed an Expand the Box training (this course or an equivalent live ETB) **and** at least one further Possibility Management training.

4.2 The CM holds current basic first aid / mental health first aid certification (or an equivalent country-recognized program): `[program name, certifying body, expiry date]`.

4.3 The CM has a named clinical supervisor or licensed mental-health professional engaged for case-by-case consultation, on paid terms, under the agreement in Template 2 of this file: `[supervisor name; Template 2 executed Y/N]`.

4.4 The CM **carries professional liability insurance appropriate for educator/coach roles in the CM's jurisdiction**, OR is employed by an entity that carries such coverage for the CM's activities under this Agreement (Doc 04 §J): `[insurer, policy number, coverage amount, expiry — or the Operator-coverage details]`. The CM will provide a certificate of insurance to the Operator on request and will notify the Operator before any lapse.

4.5 The CM will notify the Operator promptly if any of the above warranties ceases to be true (e.g., a certification lapses, the supervisor relationship ends, or insurance is cancelled). A cohort run by a CM who no longer meets Doc 04 §J is, by the framework's own statement, a cohort running without a safety framework (Doc 04 §J).

### 5. Confidentiality

5.1 The CM will hold all learner information in confidence: intake responses, screening outcomes, referral packets, direct-message exchanges, cohort-feed posts, and the contents of the pairing tracker and triage log.

5.2 Cohort-feed posts are not shared outside the cohort. Direct-message exchanges are not shared with a learner's partner or with other learners (Doc 03 §K).

5.3 **Confidentiality exceptions.** The CM may disclose otherwise-confidential information only where one of the following applies (Doc 03 §K, Doc 04 §H):
- (a) imminent risk of serious harm to the learner or another person;
- (b) legal compulsion (court order, mandatory-reporting obligation, or equivalent under applicable law); or
- (c) the learner's own explicit consent.
Any disclosure is limited to what the exception requires, and is documented (Doc 04 §G step 5).

5.4 The CM may share information with the CM's clinical supervisor for the purpose of consultation and debrief (Doc 04 §J, §L), and with the named backup CM to the extent required for coverage (Section 3.5) — both of whom are bound by equivalent confidentiality. `[Confirm the supervisor and backup are covered by NDAs or equivalent professional confidentiality.]`

5.5 Confidentiality survives termination of this Agreement indefinitely as to learner information.

### 6. Safety obligations (incorporated from Doc 03 and Doc 04)

6.1 The CM will operate the cohort in accordance with `03 - Safety and Facilitation Framework.md` and `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` as they exist at the cohort start date. These documents are incorporated into this Agreement by reference, and the CM acknowledges having read them in full.

6.2 The CM will not waive, soften, or override the gates the protocol defines as required — specifically the intake screening (Doc 04 §A), the partner-agreement requirement (Doc 04 §B), the High-module unlock checklist (Doc 04 §D), and the pre-cohort checklist (Doc 04 §K). The protocol's "skip-after-24-hours override" has been retracted (Doc 03 §C, Doc 04 §D) and the CM will not reinstate it.

6.3 The CM will **refer without flinching** (Doc 03 §K). The CM will not delay a referral in order to "help first."

6.4 The CM will maintain the documentation the framework requires: the pairing tracker (metadata only — Doc 04 §C), the triage/decision log (metadata only — `Cohort Feed Templates.md` §5), and crisis-incident records (Doc 04 §G step 5). Logs record that events occurred and what action was taken — never the content of what a learner said, except as a confidentiality exception requires.

6.5 The CM will have their own supervisor, peer, or coach to debrief with — holding a cohort is real work (Doc 03 §K). For clinical and red-flag matters, this is the supervisor under Template 2.

### 7. Liability boundaries

7.1 **The CM is not a clinician and does not provide clinical care.** The parties acknowledge that the course is educational, not therapeutic (Doc 03 §A, Doc 01), and that the CM's role is container caretaking, triage, hand-off, and documentation (Doc 03 §M, Doc 04 §G).

7.2 The CM is not responsible for clinical outcomes, for the adequacy of any third-party clinician or crisis service to which a learner is referred, or for a learner's decisions outside the course. The referral packet (Doc 04 §E) points learners to professional resources; the CM does not warrant those providers.

7.3 `[Allocation of liability, indemnification, and limitation-of-liability terms between Operator and CM — DRAFT WITH COUNSEL. These clauses are jurisdiction-specific and materially affect both parties' exposure. Do not adopt boilerplate.]`

7.4 Nothing in this Section limits any liability that cannot lawfully be limited (e.g., for fraud, or for death or personal injury caused by negligence where applicable law forbids exclusion). `[Confirm scope with counsel.]`

### 8. Insurance

8.1 The CM will maintain, for the term, professional liability insurance appropriate to educator/coach roles in the CM's jurisdiction, at a coverage level of at least `[amount]`, OR be covered under the Operator's policy for these activities (Doc 04 §J, Section 4.4). `[Specify which.]`

8.2 `[Operator's own insurance — general liability, professional liability, or equivalent — and any requirement that one party be named as additional insured. DRAFT WITH COUNSEL.]`

8.3 Each party will provide evidence of required coverage on request and will give `[notice period]` before any lapse or material change.

### 9. Data handling

9.1 The CM handles personal and sensitive personal data: contact details, region, language, intake-screening responses (including mental-health, addiction, and safety information), and communication metadata.

9.2 The CM will:
- (a) collect only the data the protocol requires (Doc 04 §A.1, §C; minimize otherwise);
- (b) store it only in the access-controlled systems that meet the privacy minimums (Doc 04 §H) — the private tracker/log spreadsheet accessible only to the CM and supervisor, and the platform components that passed §H;
- (c) not store learner content where the protocol says metadata-only (the tracker and triage log record actions, not message contents — Doc 04 §C, Feed Templates §5);
- (d) honor learner deletion requests at course end where the platform supports it (Doc 04 §H);
- (e) not transcribe voice messages with AI tools, forward learner messages publicly, or use any platform component that fails §H.

9.3 `[Applicable data-protection law (GDPR / UK GDPR / CCPA / PIPEDA / local equivalent), the lawful basis for processing, controller/processor roles as between Operator and CM, breach-notification obligations, and any required Data Processing Addendum — DRAFT WITH COUNSEL. Intake screening captures special-category / sensitive data, which most regimes regulate more strictly.]`

9.4 On termination, the CM will return or securely delete learner data per the Operator's documented retention policy and applicable law: `[retention/return/deletion terms]`.

### 10. Intellectual property and course materials

10.1 The course materials (module files, Map Notes, experiment cards, facilitator resources) remain the property of `[the Operator / the rights-holder]`. The CM is licensed to use them solely to operate covered cohorts. `[Confirm the underlying PM-content licensing chain — the course is a faithful re-presentation of an existing body of work (Doc 01) and that licensing sits upstream of this Agreement.]`

10.2 The CM's post-cohort review and operational notes are `[owned by / licensed to]` the Operator for course improvement (Doc 04 §L).

### 11. Independent judgment and escalation

11.1 The CM retains independent professional judgment in applying the safety protocol to a specific learner. Where the protocol and a learner's safety appear to conflict, **learner safety governs**, and the CM escalates to their clinical supervisor (Template 2) and, where appropriate, the Operator.

11.2 The Operator will not direct the CM to admit a learner who fails the intake screening, to skip a required gate, or to delay a referral. Any such direction is void under this Agreement, and the CM's refusal to comply with it is not a breach.

### 12. Termination

12.1 Either party may terminate this Agreement on `[notice period]` written notice.

12.2 The Operator may terminate immediately if the CM ceases to meet the Doc 04 §J qualifications (Section 4), breaches a safety obligation (Section 6) or confidentiality (Section 5), or fails to maintain required insurance (Section 8).

12.3 The CM may terminate immediately if directed to operate in a way that breaches the safety framework (Section 11.2) or applicable law.

12.4 **Mid-cohort termination.** If termination occurs mid-cohort, the parties will arrange continuity of safe coverage for enrolled learners before the CM's access ends — the named backup CM (Section 3.5) or a qualified replacement assumes the container, and in-flight red-flag situations are handed over with the documentation in Section 6.4. A cohort is not left without a qualified CM (Doc 04 §J, §K).

12.5 Sections 5 (Confidentiality), 7 (Liability boundaries), 9.4 (data return/deletion), and any accrued payment obligations survive termination.

13.1 Governing law: `[jurisdiction]`. 13.2 Dispute resolution: `[mediation / arbitration / courts — specify]`. 13.3 Entire agreement; amendments in writing signed by both parties. 13.4 Severability. 13.5 `[Other standard clauses as counsel advises.]`

## Template 2 — CM–Clinical-Supervisor Consultation Agreement

**Clinical Consultation Agreement (Community Manager Support)**

This Agreement is entered into as of `[date]` between:

- **`[CM legal name]`** ("the Community Manager" or "CM"), of `[CM address]`; and
- **`[Supervisor legal name and credentials, e.g., LCSW / clinical psychologist / psychiatrist / licensed equivalent]`** ("the Clinical Supervisor"), of `[Supervisor address / practice]`, holding license `[license type and number, jurisdiction]`.

This Agreement establishes the named clinical supervisor that `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` §J requires for a CM operating an Expand the Box cohort. The parties agree as follows.

### 1. Purpose

1.1 The CM operates the safety container for an asynchronous Possibility Management course (Doc 02, Doc 03, Doc 04). The course is educational, not clinical, and the CM is not a therapist (Doc 03 §A, §M).

1.2 The Clinical Supervisor is engaged to provide **case-by-case consultation and clinical judgment support to the CM** — so the CM has a qualified professional to consult on triage decisions, red-flag escalations, and the CM's own debrief (Doc 04 §J, §G step 5, §L; Doc 03 §K).

1.3 **The Clinical Supervisor advises the CM. The Supervisor does not treat, assess, or enter a clinical relationship with any learner.** Learners who need clinical care are referred to their own clinicians or to crisis services via the referral packet (Doc 04 §E, §G). The Supervisor's "client" is the CM's practice of running the container, not any learner.

### 2. Scope of consultation

The Clinical Supervisor will:

2.1 **Consult on triage judgment.** Help the CM apply the green/yellow/red rubric (Doc 04 §F) to ambiguous situations — where a post or DM sits between flags, or where the CM is uncertain whether a situation is in scope (emotional intensity, which is the work) or out of scope (crisis, which is referred) (Doc 03 §E, §M).

2.2 **Be available for red-flag escalation.** Serve as the CM's clinical point of contact during a crisis escalation (Doc 04 §G). Specifically, where a learner is in immediate physical danger and the CM has stayed present up to the 90-minute mark without the learner connecting to crisis services, the Supervisor is `[a / the]` designated backup the CM contacts (Doc 04 §G step 2). `[Define the Supervisor's availability for this — see Section 3.]`

2.3 **Support the post-incident debrief.** Debrief with the CM within 72 hours of any red-flag incident (Doc 04 §G step 5), and contribute clinical perspective to the post-cohort review (Doc 04 §L).

2.4 **Hold the CM's own well-being.** Provide the CM a place to process the load of holding a cohort (Doc 03 §K — "holding a cohort is real work").

2.5 The Supervisor will **not**: provide therapy, assessment, diagnosis, or treatment to any learner; contact any learner directly except as the CM and learner explicitly arrange in a specific escalation with the learner's consent; or take on the CM's documentation or decision-making responsibility (those remain the CM's — Doc 04 §G).

### 3. Availability and response

3.1 **Routine consultation:** `[cadence — e.g., a scheduled [30/60]-minute consultation [weekly / fortnightly / monthly] during an active cohort, plus ad-hoc consultation as needed]`.

3.2 **Red-flag availability:** `[the Supervisor's commitment for crisis escalation — e.g., reachable by [phone/text] during [defined hours / cohort active hours], with best-efforts response within [time]]`. The parties acknowledge that the CM's own first-response SLA to a learner red flag is 15 minutes (Doc 03 §G) and is met by the CM or the CM's named *backup CM* (a separate role — Run-Book Part 5); the Supervisor's red-flag role is the **90-minute clinical escalation point** (Doc 04 §G step 2), not the first responder, unless the parties expressly agree otherwise here: `[specify]`.

3.3 If the Supervisor is unavailable for a planned window, the Supervisor will `[give notice / name a covering clinician of equivalent qualification]`.

### 4. Boundaries (what this engagement is and is not)

4.1 This is **clinical consultation to a non-clinician operating an educational container.** It is not clinical supervision of the CM's own clinical practice (the CM has none in this role), and it does not make the Supervisor responsible for the course, the cohort, or any learner's outcome.

4.2 The Supervisor does not warrant the safety of the course or any learner's wellbeing. The Supervisor advises; the CM decides and acts within the CM's defined role; learners are responsible for their own state (Doc 03 §B).

4.3 The Supervisor's consultation does not create a clinician-patient relationship between the Supervisor and any learner, and nothing in this Agreement is to be construed as the Supervisor providing care to learners.

### 5. Confidentiality

5.1 The CM shares learner information with the Supervisor only as needed for consultation, and only to the extent permitted by the CM–Operator Agreement's confidentiality exceptions (Doc 03 §K) — consultation with one's clinical supervisor being an expressly permitted purpose (Doc 04 §J, §L).

5.2 The Supervisor holds all such information in confidence under the Supervisor's own professional and legal confidentiality obligations, and will not disclose it except as law requires or the relevant exception permits.

5.3 Where practicable, learner information shared for consultation is `[de-identified / pseudonymized]`, except where identification is necessary for an active escalation.

5.4 Confidentiality survives termination indefinitely.

6.2 Fee: `[rate — e.g., $___ per consultation hour / $___ retainer per cohort / $___ monthly]`. `[Crisis-escalation availability fee, if separate: ___.]`

6.3 Payment terms: `[schedule, invoicing, who pays — the CM personally, or the Operator on the CM's behalf]`. `[If the Operator funds this, note it; the Run-Book Part 1 Step 1 treats the supervisor relationship as a CM-readiness prerequisite regardless of who pays.]`

### Note on the two roles this file does and does not cover

This file covers the **Clinical Supervisor** (Template 2) — the qualified clinician the CM *consults*. It does **not** fully paper the **backup CM** — the person who covers the CM's unreachable windows so the 15-minute red-flag SLA holds (Doc 03 §G; Run-Book Part 5). Those are different roles: the supervisor advises on clinical judgment and is the 90-minute escalation point; the backup CM is a first responder who needs platform access and the crisis protocol. The backup CM's terms can be added as a coverage clause in Template 1 §3.5 or papered separately. Do not conflate them — a clinical supervisor on a monthly consult retainer is not automatically available to meet a 15-minute first-response standard, and a backup CM is not necessarily a licensed clinician.
