Expand the Box

A 10-module, self-paced online course in Possibility Management — the body of thoughtware developed by Clinton Callahan and the PM community. This is the May 2026 first-complete-draft edition, preserved as a reference archive. The active course is the June 2026 edition.

What this course is for

A learner who wants their thoughtware upgraded. Specifically: the Box becomes visible enough to disidentify from; the four feelings become distinct archetypal energies rather than “things to manage”; the Low Drama Triangle becomes detectable in real time; listening and speaking become acts of possibility; the three powers — choosing, asking dangerous questions, declaring — become operational.

The course delivers this through 10 self-paced modules, async partner pairings, embodied practices, between-module experiments, and a structured safety container.

This course is not therapy. Not coaching. Not trauma treatment. It is an educational course in PM thoughtware.

Before you open Day 1

Read Start Here (Learner Welcome) first — that single page is your whole orientation (the rhythm, the agreements, the grounding practice, what to do if it gets to be too much). It takes about fifteen minutes.

Learner checklist:
  1. Read Start Here (≈15 min)
  2. Complete intake screening (your community manager sends it)
  3. Confirm you have your region’s referral packet within reach
  4. Get paired with a partner
  5. Open Day 1 — Orientation, New Context, Radical Responsibility

You do not need to read the operator docs (the numbered .md files in the root) — those are for community managers.

Provenance and lineage

All Possibility Management distinctions in this course originate with Clinton Callahan and the PM community since the late 1980s. This course re-presents those distinctions in clean facilitator language for async delivery. The course does not reproduce source videos, transcripts, or photographed maps from the source library.

Course version 1.0 — first complete draft (May 2026). The locked templates and working agreements cannot change mid-cohort; see the operator docs for the full structure.
