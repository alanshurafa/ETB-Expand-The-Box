# Start Here

*Read this once before you open Day 1. It's the only orientation you need. Fifteen minutes.*

Welcome to Expand the Box. This page tells you how the course actually works — the rhythm, the people, the safety net, and what to do on your first day. The Day modules teach Possibility Management. This page teaches you how to *take* the course.

---

## What this is, in one breath

Ten modules over about thirty days. You watch short videos, read a tightly-written module, do one solo practice, exchange a voice message with one partner, run one small experiment in your actual life, and write a few reflections. Then you do it again, nine more times. The work is not in the watching. It's in the practices and the experiments — the small reps you run between the videos.

It is **not** therapy, coaching, or treatment. It is an education in a set of distinctions and maps. It will sometimes be uncomfortable — that's the work — but discomfort that overwhelms you is your cue to pause and reach for support, not to push through. More on that below.

---

## The rhythm of one module

You move through one module every **~3 days**. There's no clock forcing you; this is the shape that works:

- **Day one of the cycle:** watch the module's video(s), read the module, do the solo practice (~2 hours on lighter days, ~3 on the heavier ones — each module's header tells you its real time).
- **Across the next day or two:** run the *between-module experiment* — a small specific rep you do inside your ordinary life (a conversation you were going to have anyway, a meeting, dinner). It costs no extra scheduled time.
- **Before the next module:** send your partner a voice message, listen to theirs, write your reflections, post one line to the cohort feed.

Faster is fine (≈20 days). Slower is fine (≈60). What matters is the rhythm — *content → practice → experiment → reflection* — not the speed.

---

## The intensity tags

Every module is tagged **Low**, **Medium**, or **High**. The tag is in the module header and it's honest about what the day asks of you.

- **Low** (Days 1, 3, 4, 10): mostly cognitive and embodied-light. *One exception:* **Day 3 is Low emotional intensity but it is load-bearing** — it installs the centering, grounding, and bubble practices that every hard day later stands on. Don't skim Day 3.
- **Medium** (Days 2, 8): real but manageable charge.
- **High** (Days 5, 6, 7, 9): these directly engage your feelings, your drama patterns, and your shadow. They can surface material you haven't met before. They require an active partner and a consent check before you begin. Take them when you have time, privacy, and your partner is reachable — not at the end of a hard day.

The three High modules in a row — **Days 5, 6, 7** — are the heaviest stretch of the course. There's a built-in integration pause between Day 6 and Day 7. Use it. Don't power through.

## The four agreements

Beginning Day 1 means agreeing to four things. They're repeated at the top of each module. They are what make the work safe.

1. **Choice is always available.** You can pause, skip a practice, slow down, take a longer break, or stop entirely. Nothing here is mandatory. No part of this course requires you to push through anything. If your body says no, the answer is no.
2. **Consent is ongoing.** Each High module asks your consent again before it begins. Agreeing once at enrollment doesn't carry forward into every exercise. You can decline any single practice and keep going.
3. **You are responsible for your own state.** The course gives you the practices — center, ground, drop a bubble, feel, choose. You use them. If you can't reach them in a moment, you pause and reach for support.
4. **Your partner is alive.** Before the High modules (5, 6, 7, 9), you confirm your partner is reachable. If they're not, the module waits. This isn't a suggestion — it's the structure.

## Your partner

You'll be matched with one other learner for the whole course. You exchange voice messages — usually 3–8 minutes each way, once per module. They are your witness, not your therapist, coach, or fixer. What they do: listen, reflect back what they actually heard, ask you one open question. What they don't do: advise, diagnose, or fix.

You don't need to be good at this yet — the course teaches the listening and speaking on Day 8. Until then, "I heard you say… here's what moved in me… here's one question I'm leaving with you" is enough.

**If your partner goes quiet:** tell your community manager (see below). There's a real fallback — a witness partner or a CM-held exchange — so a silent partner never strands you. You don't have to solve it alone, and you don't have to wait indefinitely.

---

## The cohort feed

A shared space where everyone posts a line or two after each module — what landed, what's sticking sideways, an optional question. Keep it brief; the deep processing goes to your partner, not the feed. Don't post other people's stories. Don't give advice. It's a place to be witnessed by the group, lightly.

---

## Your community manager (CM)

One person holds the container for your cohort. They're reachable by message — within 24 hours for ordinary things, **immediately** for anything urgent. Reach the CM when: your partner isn't working, something in your life changed that affects whether you should continue, you want a referral, or you're worried about yourself or someone else in the cohort.

The CM is not your therapist. They keep the container intact and point you to the right support. That's a real and skilled job — it's just not clinical care.

## When it gets to be too much

This is the part to read twice.

**The 60-second grounding practice** — use it any time you notice you're floating, can't follow the words, your heart is racing without cause, you're watching yourself from outside, or you have a strong urge to flee, attack, or shut down:

> 1. Stop. Close the video or the page.
> 2. Stand up. Both feet on the floor. Knees soft.
> 3. Breathe out longer than you breathe in — three times. Out loud is fine.
> 4. Name three things you can see.
> 5. Name one thing you can hear.
> 6. One hand on your chest, one on your belly. Notice your body is here.
> 7. Then decide: continue, pause ten minutes, or stop for today.

The interactive tools on the High days have a **GROUND NOW** button that opens this script with one tap. The script lives at the top of every module too.

**Three levels of "this is hard":**
- Discomfort that is *moving* → keep going gently. This is the work.
- Discomfort that is *stuck or escalating* → ground, pause at least ten minutes, then re-decide.
- Discomfort that is *unmistakably crisis* → stop, contact your partner, and if needed the referral list. If you or someone else may be in immediate danger, contact local emergency services or your country's crisis line **now** — don't wait for the course.

You received a **referral packet** when you enrolled — crisis lines, clinicians, and coaches for your region. Keep it somewhere you can reach in thirty seconds. If you don't have it, ask your CM before you start Day 1.

**This course is not a substitute for therapy or medical care.** If what surfaces is bigger than a thoughtware course is built to hold, that's not you doing it wrong — it's the signal to bring in someone qualified. The course will still be here.

## A few words you'll meet on Day 1

You don't need to study these — they're taught as you go. Just so they're not strangers:

- **The Box** — the survival-and-protection thoughtware you assembled as a kid. The whole course turns on one line: *you have a Box, you are not your Box.*
- **Thoughtware** — what you think *with* (the operating system), as distinct from what you think *about*.
- **Liquid state** — the loosened state in which thoughtware can actually change.
- **Gremlin** — the part of you that feeds on drama. Met directly later.
- **Beep! Book** — a literal notebook you'll start on Day 4 as your engineering log of small experiments. Have a notebook ready.
- **Experiment** — a small, specific, real-life rep with a clear *what I'll do / by when / what I'll notice*. The reps compound.
