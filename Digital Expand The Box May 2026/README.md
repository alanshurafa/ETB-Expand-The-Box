# Expand the Box — Async Online Course

A 10-module, self-paced online course in **Possibility Management** — the body of thoughtware developed by Clinton Callahan and the PM community.

This folder contains the complete course: architecture, frameworks, ten self-contained modules (each teaching its maps inline), twenty-nine Map Note cards, a `Maps/` image set, a `Map Atlas/` of 29 interactive map tools, facilitator resources, and a single-file assembled version.

## What this course is for

A learner who wants their thoughtware upgraded. Specifically:
- The Box becomes visible enough that the learner can disidentify from it
- The four feelings (anger, sadness, fear, joy) become distinct, useful, archetypal energies rather than "things to manage"
- The Low Drama Triangle becomes detectable in real time
- Listening and speaking become acts of possibility, not transmission
- The three powers — choosing, asking dangerous questions, declaring — become operational

The course does this through 10 self-paced modules, async partner pairings, embodied practices, between-module experiments, and a structured safety container.

The course is **not** therapy. Not coaching. Not trauma treatment. Not psychiatric care. It is an educational course in PM thoughtware. See `03 - Safety and Facilitation Framework.md` for the full distinction.

## Reading order

### For a learner about to take the course
**Read `00 - START HERE (Learner Welcome).md` — that one page is your whole orientation** (how the course works, the agreements, the grounding practice, what to do if it gets to be too much). You do **not** need to read this README or the operator docs. Then, with your community manager:
1. Read `00 - START HERE (Learner Welcome).md` (≈15 min)
2. Complete intake screening (your CM sends it)
3. Confirm you have your region's referral packet
4. Get paired with a partner
5. Open `Days/Day 01 …`

*(Sections B, C, D, F, I of `03 - Safety and Facilitation Framework.md` are the operator's source for the safety practices; the Start-Here page already assembles what a learner needs from them, in learner voice.)*

### For a community manager preparing to run a cohort
1. Read this README in full
2. Read `01 - Course Architecture and Pedagogy.md` in full
3. Read `02 - Async Delivery Framework.md` in full
4. Read `03 - Safety and Facilitation Framework.md` cover to cover
5. Read **`04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` in full — this is the operational protocol; the cohort cannot open without it**
6. Read `Facilitator Resources/Async Partner Pairing Playbook.md` and `Cohort Feed Templates.md`
7. Skim Day 1, Day 5, Day 10 to understand the arc
8. Complete the pre-cohort checklist (Doc 04 Section K)
9. Open the cohort

### For a course designer or contributor
1. Read 00 (Source Inventory) — what the course was built from
2. Read 01 (Architecture) — why the course is shaped the way it is, especially the "Key distinctions baked into the architecture" section
3. Read the locked templates in 01 (Module shape, Map Note shape)
4. Read Day 1, Day 5, Day 10 as voice anchors
5. Skim a few Map Notes (M01, M10, M13, M23 cover the range)
6. Then contribute

## Course architecture in one glance

| Phase | Modules | What gets installed |
|---|---|---|
| **Container & frame** | 1, 2 | New context · the chain · the Box as filter · radical responsibility |
| **Embodiment infrastructure** | 3, 4 | Liquid state · center/ground/bubble · five bodies · feedback · coaching · experiments |
| **Emotional body** | 5, 6 | Feelings vs emotions · old map · numbness bar · mixed emotions · emotional healing process |
| **Relational body** | 7, 8 | Low Drama · gremlin food · listening · speaking · completion loops |
| **Integration** | 9, 10 | Ego states · problem ownership · learning spiral · map of possibility · bright principles · three powers |

Each module is one file in `Days/`. Each module follows a 14-section locked template (header → purpose → core concepts → learning outcomes → maps → flow → teaching notes → embodied practice → partner exchange → between-module experiment → reflection prompts → safety callouts → cohort feed post → glossary additions).

Each map has a one-page **Map Note card** in `Map Notes/` (29 in total) — the embedded ETB map, a short "what it is / at a glance" summary, and links to its day module and its interactive **Map Atlas** tool. The full teaching now lives in the day modules (where each map is taught inline) and in the Map Atlas. M01–M23 follow the day arc; M24–M29 are supplementary maps wired into the days they support (Day 1, 4, 7, 9, 10).

## The Map Atlas

`Map Atlas/` holds 29 standalone interactive map-teaching tools — one per map, M01 through M29 — opened from `Map Atlas/index.html`. Each tool walks the learner through a single map in the browser, made clickable and self-paced rather than only read. It is the interactive companion to the Day modules and to the slimmed-down Map Note cards, so a learner can either read a map or work through it. The Day pages under `Interactive Tools/` link straight to the matching Atlas tool for every map they install.

## Pacing

The default cadence is **one module per ~3 days, ~30 days total**. See `02 - Async Delivery Framework.md` Section I for cadence variants. The course is async; the learner sets the pace within their cohort's container.

## Safety, in one sentence

The container is real, the safety practices are explicit, the screening is the gate (not a recommendation), the partner pairing is structural, the referral pathway is one click away from any module, and the course does not pretend to be appropriate for everyone. See `03` and `04` for the full structure.

## A note on what's deliberately left out

- No badges, streaks, completion certificates, or gamification — PM does not reward compliance
- No replay-able quizzes — PM is verified in lived experience
- No public discussion forum for "questions about PM" — that conversation happens elsewhere; the cohort feed stays narrow
- No app — a platform is enough

The course's force is in the modules, the partner pairings, and the experiments the learner runs in their actual life. Everything else is infrastructure.
