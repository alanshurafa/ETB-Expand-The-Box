# M23 — Three Powers (Choose, Ask Dangerous Questions, Declare)

*The operational moves that make possibility actionable — owning the choice you already made, asking the question whose answer you cannot un-know, and speaking the new context into being from a clean source.*

![Map of 3 Powers — Possibility Management map](../Maps/M23.png)

**What it is.** The Map of Possibility (M20) names a space; bright and shadow principles (M21) name what sources the action inside it; the Three Powers name the moves you can make from there. They are not techniques but sequenced stances: Choosing is the foundation, Asking Dangerous Questions populates the space with the previously invisible, and Declaring is the speech act that brings a new context into being and walks the speaker into it. Most "personal growth" reaches for declaration without first owning the choice — so the declarations don't hold.

**At a glance.** Choosing → own that you chose, even on autopilot (without it, the other two don't exist) · Asking Dangerous Questions → questions whose answer you cannot un-know; the engine of possibility · Declaring → a speech act creating a new context (not a promise, opinion, or wish) · Declarations sourced from bright create; sourced from gremlin/Box collapse · Sequential, not parallel · Quiet declarations can outweigh loud ones — volume is not the marker.
