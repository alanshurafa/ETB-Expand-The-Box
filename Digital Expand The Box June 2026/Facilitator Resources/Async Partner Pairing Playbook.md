# Async Partner Pairing Playbook

| | |
|---|---|
| **For** | Community Manager (CM) running an async Expand the Box cohort |
| **When** | Open this file before matching begins and keep it open through cohort close |
| **Replaces** | The in-person "Breakout Room Designs" document |
| **Reads alongside** | `02 - Async Delivery Framework.md` Section C · `03 - Safety and Facilitation Framework.md` Sections B, F · `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` Sections A, B, C, D, I |

The pairing IS the container — the energetic infrastructure that lets the learner stay in their body while their thoughtware is upgrading. Without a working pairing, the modules become information. With one, transformation. Treat the pairing as load-bearing.

Doc 04 holds the protocols (partner agreement, tracker fields, unlock checklist, mismatch logic). This playbook holds the language — verbatim CM voice messages, per-module exchange shapes, and the decision trees that keep the container intact.

---

## 1. Pre-cohort — matching learners

Matching happens before content access opens. A learner cannot start Module 1 without an assigned partner. Late enrollees: match first, open content second.

**Inputs** (from Doc 04 Section A.1): time zone and weekly availability windows · primary language · cadence declaration (Default / Compressed / Stretched / Pulsed) · country and state/province.

**Rules, in priority order.**

1. **Cadence first.** Same cadence variant only. Never match a Compressed learner with a Stretched learner — you will be re-matching by Day 4.
2. **Time zone within 6 hours.** A 24h SLA dies fast when one partner is asleep two-thirds of the window. If two cadence-matched learners are >6h apart, prefer a less-cadence-matched pair inside 6h.
3. **Shared primary language.** Voice messages carry tone; tone does not translate. Same language only.
4. **No pre-existing close ties.** Spouses, business partners, very close friends bring entanglement that compromises the practice. If unavoidable in a small cohort, name the dynamic explicitly and add a witness partner from the start (Section 6).
5. **Algorithmic fallback.** Two equally-good matches: pair the earlier-enrolled learner with the closer-availability partner. The later learner gets second-best. Latecomers do not bump earlier learners.

**Do not match on** age, gender, profession, content interest, "vibe." None of these predict whether the pairing will produce a real exchange by Day 4. Cadence does.

**Solo learners.** Odd-numbered cohort: the CM is the partner for the leftover learner for the first week only, then re-match when the next cohort starts or a witness partner volunteers (Section 6). The CM as partner is a stopgap — it compromises triage neutrality.

## 2. The partner-pairing welcome email

CM sends within 24h of matching, BCC'd to both partners. Voice-message-ready; the CM can also record and send a voice version.

```
Subject: Your Expand the Box partner — [Partner B's first name]

Hi [A] and [B],

You two are paired for this cohort. You are not each other's
therapist, coach, or fixer. You are each other's witness. The course
teaches witnessing step by step; for now — listen, reflect what you
heard, ask one honest question. That is the whole job.

BEFORE YOU EXCHANGE ANYTHING, both of you sign the partner agreement
below. Reply-all "signed" to confirm. A learner who has not signed
is not eligible for pairing, and pairing is required to unlock the
course.

The partner agreement (verbatim):

  1. I will respond to my partner's voice message within 24 hours,
     even if briefly.
  2. I will not advise, fix, or diagnose. I will witness.
  3. I will hold what my partner shares in confidence.
  4. If my partner tells me they may harm themselves or someone else,
     I will ask: "Are you in immediate danger right now?" If yes,
     I will contact local emergency or crisis services AND the CM
     immediately. I will not wait for course protocol.
  5. If I become unable to be in the partnership, I will tell the CM
     within 48 hours so a re-match happens.
  6. I will not use what my partner shares in any other context.
  7. I will not enter a sexual, financial, or professional
     relationship with my partner during the course.

PLATFORM. Pick a private 1:1 voice-message tool. Acceptable:
Telegram, WhatsApp (E2EE on), Voxer, Signal. Not acceptable: Discord
servers, group chats, anything that auto-transcribes with AI.
Confirm to me which you chose.

FIRST EXCHANGE — the Day 1 partner prompt (attached as PDF).
Record 3-8 minutes: who you are · what is alive in you about being
here · what you specifically came for. Listen to your partner's
message all the way through, then reply 3-8 minutes: what you heard ·
what you noticed in yourself while listening · one open question.
Speak from your body. Stumbles stay in. Do not edit.

DEADLINES.
  - Within 24 hours: agreement signed.
  - Within 48 hours: platform chosen.
  - Within 72 hours: first voice message sent.
  - If your partner stops responding for >24 hours or the pairing
    feels unworkable — tell me, not them. Silent unworkable pairings
    are worse than swapped ones.

I do not read your exchanges. I track only that they happened. The
only confidentiality exception is agreement item 4.

— [CM name]
```

Attachments: partner agreement PDF, the Day 1 partner prompt PDF, and the per-learner referral packet re-attached as a reminder.

## 3. Per-module partner-exchange template

The exchange shape stays the same across all 10 modules. The prompt content lives in each `Day NN - …` file under "Partner exchange (async)." The CM does not author per-module prompts — points to them, confirms the shape is held.

### Generic shape (every module)

```
A records 3-8 min (5-10 min for High modules):
  - Responds to the module's three-point partner prompt
  - Speaks from the body, not from a script; stumbles stay in
  - Voice message only (or scheduled call — Doc 02 Section C.3)

B listens within 24h, all the way through once, then records 3-7 min:
  1. What I heard you say — the part that landed
  2. What I noticed in myself while listening — feeling, body 
     sensation, thought
  3. One open question I'm leaving with you — lands in the body

NO advice, NO fixing, NO "have you tried," NO "I had a similar 
experience." Witnessing only. Optional second round if either wants.
```

### Three worked examples

**Day 1 (Low) — Orientation, New Context, Radical Responsibility.** Prompt: who you are · what is alive in you about being in this course · what you specifically came for. The first exchange is almost always awkward. The welcome email pre-warns ("awkwardness is not danger; first reps are clumsy") so neither partner reads the awkwardness as a bad sign.

**Day 5 (High) — Feelings vs Emotions, Old Map of Feelings, Numbness Bar.** Prompt: one specific rule from your Old Map of Feelings (e.g., *"in my family, anger meant my father had been drinking, so I learned: if you feel angry, leave the room"*) · what surfaced in the Low-Intensity Feelings practice (which feelings registered with which objects, whether you hit the Numbness Bar) · one feeling alive in you right now in the form *"Right now I feel some [feeling], the sensation is [location and quality], what I think it is about is [one sentence]."* Reply: what you heard · what feeling surfaced in you while listening, named/located/intensity-noted — *"I felt some sadness in my throat, low intensity, maybe 10%"* · one open question. If a partner names an emotion (feeling about something years ago, story attached, >5 minutes), the listener gently names it as out-of-scope and points to the CM. Day 6 is for emotional healing process; Day 5 is for registering.

**Day 8 (Medium) — Listening, Speaking, Communication, Completion Loops.** The exchange IS the module. Partners schedule 45 min synchronous if possible. Each speaks 5 min about something genuinely alive; the other holds Possibility Listening (no nodding, no smiling, no questions). At the 5-min mark the listener replies with exactly three statements: *"What I heard you say was…" · "What I noticed in myself was…" · "The question I am leaving with you is…"* Then they reverse. Both end with the explicit completion loop: *"Are we complete on this exchange?" — "Yes."* The CM sends a 24-hour-advance reminder DM: *"Day 8 is the relational practice module. The exchange IS the module. Find 45 min with your partner this week."*

## 4. The 24-hour SLA enforcement protocol

The pairing tracker (Doc 04 Section C) flags missed SLAs mechanically. The trigger rules do the remembering for the CM.

**Trigger 1 — one missed SLA in a single module.** No action. Life happens; the pairing self-corrects most of the time.

**Trigger 2 — missed SLA twice in one module.** CM voice-messages BOTH partners (not just the silent one). Same message to both:

```
Hi [name] — cadence dropped on Module [N] with [partner]. Not 
asking what's in the exchange — I don't read those. Checking the 
structure: is the pairing still working?

Three options:
  1. Fine, life was just full — we'll catch up. Reply "fine."
  2. Something's off but workable — tell me, we'll talk.
  3. Not working — I'll re-match. No story needed.

Reply by [date, ~48h]. No reply = option 3.
```

**Trigger 3 — missed SLA in two consecutive modules.** CM offers a re-match. Same message to both:

```
Hi [name] — cadence has dropped two modules in a row with [partner]. 
That's the pattern where I step in.

I'm re-matching unless you tell me by [date, 48h] that you have a 
specific plan to get back on rhythm. No judgment. The modules ahead 
([list 2 upcoming]) lean on the pairing being live.

Re-match, or specific plan?
```

**Trigger 4 — one partner has not initiated in 6 days.** CM unilaterally offers the silent partner's responsive partner a re-match:

```
Hi [name] — [partner] hasn't initiated in 6 days. That leaves you 
without a working witness, and you have [Module N: a High] coming. 
I'm re-matching you. The other learner stays in the cohort; the 
silence is between them and me.

New partner within 48h. Same 7-line agreement and platform setup 
apply. If you'd rather wait a few more days for [original partner], 
tell me. Default is re-match.
```

## 5. The re-match protocol

Re-matching is not failure. It is the container self-correcting. The CM frames it that way every time.

**When to re-match.**
- Trigger 2 (Section 4), if either partner chose option 3
- Trigger 3, if no specific plan
- Trigger 4, default
- Either partner requests it, with or without a reason
- A Yellow or Red flag in either partner's DMs names the pairing as part of the problem
- A pre-existing close-tie pairing turns out to be too entangled (Section 1, rule 4)

**Who to re-match the now-single learner to**, in order of preference: another single learner from a re-match elsewhere in the cohort · a learner whose original partner withdrew · a waitlisted learner from the same cohort · a witness partner from the bench (Section 6) for one module until a permanent re-match arrives.

**Language to the re-paired learners.** Use the welcome email template (Section 2), replacing the opener with:

```
Hi [A] and [B],

Re-pairing — you two are now paired for the rest of this cohort. 
Re-matching is normal; about one in four cohorts has one. It is 
not a failure of either of you or the prior pair. Cadence is 
something you only see after the first few reps.

[rest of the welcome email]
```

**Language to the partner being re-matched away from.** Brief, no story, no blame:

```
Hi [name] — I'm re-matching [partner]. You stay in the cohort. 
You will have a new partner by [date]. Nothing is owed to me by 
way of explanation.
```

The CM never tells one partner what the other said about the re-match. Confidentiality holds across the re-match boundary.

## 6. The witness-partner substitute

When partners drift more than one module apart for 4+ consecutive days (Doc 04 Section I), the slower learner faces a High module without a live witness and the faster learner stalls waiting. The witness partner is the bridge.

**Who.** In priority order: a graduate of a prior cohort on the witness bench (CM maintains a small list, 2-4 people, refreshed each cohort) · a current cohort learner whose own partner is briefly off · the CM for one exchange only (last resort — compromises triage neutrality).

**When to deploy.** Cadence mismatch >1 module for 4+ days blocking the faster learner's next High module · original partner has a known temporary absence crossing a High-module window · a re-match is in progress and the new pairing has not yet built relational ground for a High module.

**What the witness partner does.** Exactly one exchange — the upcoming module's prompt, the standard three-point shape. They read the partner agreement and the module's partner-exchange prompt before recording. Not a long-term replacement; a one-rep bridge.

**Language to the learner needing the witness:**

```
Hi [name] — bringing in a witness partner for Module [N] so your 
pairing stays intact. [Witness name] is a [prior-cohort graduate / 
current-cohort learner whose partner is briefly off]. They have 
signed the agreement.

Record your Module [N] prompt and send to them on [platform]. They 
reply within 24h under the standard shape. After that, continue 
with your original partner.

Structural, not a re-match. Tell [original partner] or not — your 
call.
```

**Language to the witness partner:**

```
Hi [name] — one exchange for [learner], Module [N]. They are 
otherwise paired; partner briefly off-rhythm. Standard shape: 
receive their voice message, listen all the way through, reply 3-7 
min with what you heard / what you noticed / one question.

[Attach the Day NN partner prompt.]

Confidentiality is real. The container you held in your own cohort 
extends here.
```

## 7. High-module partner check-in script

Before unlocking a High module (Days 5, 6, 7, 9), the unlock checklist (Doc 04 Section D) requires the learner confirm *"My partner is reachable today and tomorrow."* This is not the learner asserting it in their head — it is the result of a specific exchange, 24-48 hours ahead of the module. Verbatim language the learner sends:

```
Hi [partner] — I'm about to start Module [N], a High one. Course 
rule: I confirm with you that you're reachable the next 48h before 
I unlock it.

If I send you a voice message any time in the next two days — end 
of module, during the practice, between — you'll get back within 
the 24h SLA. Same as always, just confirming.

If you can hold that — voice-reply "yes, I'm here, go." 
If you can't — also fine, just tell me. I'll wait or ask the CM 
for a witness partner. No story needed.
```

The partner replies, voice, brief: *"Yes, I'm here, go."* Or *"I can't this window, here's why."* No platform confirmation = no unlock. If the partner cannot hold it, the module waits or a witness partner is deployed (Section 6). The course will still be there in a week.

## 8. Closing the partnership

Day 10 closes the pairing as a course structure — explicit closure, not fade. Whatever happens after Day 10 between the learners is a different gameworld with different rules.

**The closure exchange (Day 10 partner prompt — verbatim from the module).** Each partner records 5-8 min: what you are taking forward (1-2 specific 90-day practices, not aspirational) · the declaration (one of the three Day 10 declarations, said as a declaration, out loud into the recording — partner is witness) · your sourcing (one bright principle you sourced from; one shadow principle you caught yourself sourcing from — no moralizing) · one thing you want your partner to know (gratitude, apology, question, thing left unsaid). Each replies 5-8 min: repeat the declaration verbatim into the field · what you noticed while listening · the piece of them you are carrying forward · a sentence that closes the pairing cleanly so it does not leak into ambiguity.

**Opt-in continuation.** After the closure exchange, the CM sends a private message to each partner separately:

```
Hi [name] — cohort closes [date]. The pairing as a course structure 
closes with it.

If you and [partner] want to continue beyond the cohort, that's 
between you, outside my container. I do not track post-cohort 
exchanges and do not mediate post-cohort issues.

Two things to weigh:
  - Item 7 (no sexual / financial / professional relationship) ends 
    with the cohort — no longer prohibited, also no longer 
    structurally surfaced for examination. Be deliberate.
  - The 24h SLA was structural to this course, not a promise either 
    of you owes the other for life.

Continue cleanly or close cleanly. Either is good.
```

## 9. Troubleshooting

The eight scenarios you will see most often, with what to do.

**1. The ghost — one partner stops responding entirely.**

After 6 days of no initiated exchange, default to a re-match for the responsive partner (Trigger 4, Section 4). Do not chase the silent partner more than twice. The silence is data; respect it. If the silent partner re-emerges later wanting to rejoin, the CM offers a witness-bench slot, not a new pairing — they re-earn the container's trust by witnessing before they are witnessed.

**2. The carrier — one partner is doing all the work.**

Voice-message the carrier privately: *"I notice you're holding more of the container than [partner] is right now. The pairing isn't built for that. Want me to surface it with them, or do you want to surface it yourself first?"* If the carrier wants to surface it themselves, the CM gives them the Day 8 completion-loop frame: name it cleanly, ask for what they need, close the loop. If they want the CM to surface it, the CM voice-messages the other partner with the standard cadence-drop language (Section 4, Trigger 2). Re-match if the imbalance persists past one more module.

**3. The romantic interest — one partner becomes attracted to the other.**

Item 7 forbids a sexual relationship during the course. The attraction itself is not the violation; acting on it during the cohort is. CM voice-messages the partner who surfaced the interest:

```
Hi [name] — what you noticed is real. The container sometimes generates 
this; you are not the first. Item 7 holds for the cohort. Notice the 
energy; do not act on it inside the course. After Day 10 you and 
[partner] decide what is between you in your own gameworld.

If the attraction makes it hard to hold a clean witnessing stance, 
tell me and I will re-match. Not punishment — choosing the container 
over the shape it took.
```

If mutual and announced to the CM by both, re-match both. They cannot witness each other cleanly inside an unspoken charged field. The romantic path is theirs after Day 10.

**4. The advice spiral — partner asks for advice the other can't give.**

The asking partner is bumping against a real edge. The asked partner holds the structure:

```
"What you're asking for is advice, and the agreement says I don't 
advise. What I can do is witness — tell me more about what's under 
the question. Or: this might be a CM moment, want me to flag it?"
```

If the asker keeps pushing for advice, CM voice-messages them:

```
Hi [name] — the pairing is built for witnessing, not advising. You 
keep landing on advice. That is data — either you need a different 
conversation than the pairing was built for (coach, clinician, 
referral list), or the witnessing shape is too uncomfortable to stay 
inside. Both are workable. Tell me which.
```

**5. The out-of-scope share — partner shares Red-flag material.**

Active suicidal ideation, abuse in progress, psychotic episode, severe addiction relapse — anything matching a Red flag in the triage rubric (Doc 04 Section F). The listening partner follows the buddy immediate-danger protocol (Doc 03 Section F): *"Are you in immediate danger right now?"* If yes — crisis line + CM, immediately. If no — voice-message the CM the same day. The listening partner is the relay, not the clinician.

**6. The theory debate — one partner wants to argue PM concepts.**

The pairing is not for that. The cohort feed is not for that. CM voice-messages the debater:

```
Hi [name] — the partner exchanges hold a specific shape: witness, 
reflect, ask one question. Sitting with confusion about a concept 
and voice-messaging your partner about what's hard to swallow — 
yes. Arguing whether the four levels are "really" four — different 
forum.

PM communities exist for that and I can point you. For the next 
30 days, the pairing is for the practice.
```

**7. The fixer — partner can't help advising even after being asked not to.**

The fixer is running an Old Map. The CM does not try to fix the fixer (that's the recursion they're caught in). Names the pattern once, gently:

```
Hi [name] — I heard advice came through your last exchange. Not a 
moral problem; a thoughtware one. The witnessing shape is harder 
than it sounds, especially if "helping" is how you've been useful 
your whole life. Day 8 works this directly.

Until then: in your next reply, the only three things you say back 
are "what I heard," "what I noticed," "one question." Anything 
else, leave out. Even if it feels withholding. The discomfort of 
not advising is the practice.
```

If the fixer persists past Day 8, the issue is no longer skill — it is fit. Re-match.

**8. The dropout — one partner withdraws mid-course.**

The remaining partner is re-matched (Section 5). The withdrawing partner closes via a brief CM exchange: acknowledge the choice, confirm refund/pause per cohort terms, confirm agreement items 3, 6, and 7 remain in force as honored ethics; items 1, 4, and 5 end with their cohort access. The remaining partner is told only that the partner withdrew, not why.

---

The container is built one exchange at a time. The CM's job is not to make the exchanges happen — that is between the partners. The CM's job is to make sure the conditions for the exchanges to happen are in place: the agreement signed, the platform set, the cadence matched, the unlock checklist gated, the silence noticed before it becomes a default. Do those, and the container holds. The course does the rest.
