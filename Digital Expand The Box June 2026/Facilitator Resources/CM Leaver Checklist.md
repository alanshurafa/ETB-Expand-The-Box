# CM Leaver Checklist

*Operator-facing, half a page. Run it whenever a learner stops, silently or by telling you. Mechanics live in the Async Partner Pairing Playbook (§4–6, §9); this is the order of operations.*

---

## If the leave is silent

- [ ] **Chase at most twice.** Two voice messages, spaced about three days (the Playbook's SLA triggers do the timing). Then stop. The silence is data; respect it. No third message, no guilt copy.
- [ ] **Protect the partner.** At six days without an initiated exchange, re-match the responsive partner (Playbook Trigger 4; the default, not an escalation).
- [ ] **Close the pairing without blame.** The leaver stays formally in the cohort; access stays open; no removal email, no announcement. If the leaver resurfaces, route them to re-entry terms below.

## If the leave is declared

- [ ] **Leaver wellbeing check** (voice message or call, this script or close to it):

  > "Thank you for telling me — that is stopping well, not failing. Two questions before I close things cleanly. One: are you stopping because life is full, or because something in the course material knocked you over? If it's the second, tell me which module and I'll point you at the right support; that's my job, not a judgment. Two: is anything open right now (a stirred-up emotion, an unfinished exchange) that needs a hand before you put this down?"

  Anything Red in the answers (ideation, abuse disclosure, crisis) → triage rubric Red: same-day response, crisis lines in the first message.
- [ ] **Offer the pause before the close.** "Pause and resume next cohort" is on the table explicitly; the red-pill ceremony promised it (*"I can stop at any time"*). The course will still be here in three months — say so.
- [ ] **Close their pairing without blame.** Tell the remaining partner only the structure: the pairing is closed, a re-match is coming, nothing about why. Use the Playbook's no-story language; confidentiality holds across the boundary.

## Both paths

- [ ] **Stopping-well framing, said out loud.** Stopping well is a valid use of the container, not a failure of it. A learner who leaves cleanly returns at roughly the rate of one who never left badly; a learner shamed at the exit does not return at all.
- [ ] **Archive what gets archived.** Pairing-tracker row marked closed (structure only; the CM never logged exchange content). Cohort-feed posts stay until the standard 14-day post-cohort archive. The referral packet stays delivered. Nothing the learner would need for re-entry is deleted.
- [ ] **State the re-entry terms at leaving time**, so coming back is concrete, not vague goodwill:
  - The learner-facing path is [Coming Back](../Practice/Coming%20Back.md) (3+/7+/30+ day tiers, anti-binge rule, High-module rule).
  - If their pairing was closed, re-entry runs through the **witness bench**: they witness one exchange before they are witnessed (Playbook §9: the ghost re-earns matrix trust by witnessing first). A declared leaver who stopped well re-enters on the same bench terms if their partner has been re-matched; nothing else is re-earned.
- [ ] **Log it for the post-cohort review:** leave date, module reached, silent/declared, re-entry terms stated. Structure, not story.
