# Cohort Calendar Template

| | |
|---|---|
| **For** | The operator and CM, planning one cohort end to end |
| **What it is** | A fill-in calendar covering Modules 00–11, the pause beats, teach-backs, the mid-course call, and the 30/60/90 alumni touchpoints, with every SLA-relevant date marked for the CM |
| **Reads alongside** | `02 - Async Delivery Framework.md` (cadence) · `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` (the gates this calendar schedules) · [CM First-Cohort Run-Book](CM%20First-Cohort%20Run-Book.md) · [Cohort Feed Templates](Cohort%20Feed%20Templates.md) |

**How to use it.** Pick a start date (the day content access opens; a Monday works well so the heaviest stretch avoids landing on a learner's worst workdays). Fill the "Your dates" column top to bottom. The "Worked example" column shows a cohort opening **Monday 5 October 2026** at the recommended default cadence. Give every learner the filled calendar in the welcome packet, and pin it in the cohort feed.

**The cadence this template assumes.** Default pace: one module per **~3 days** (Doc 02 §A and §I). Modules are multi-day units, not daily lessons: a module's reading and practice land on its first day, the between-module experiment runs during the following days, the partner exchange closes the cycle. Faster (2 days/module) and stretched (4–6 days/module) variants exist; stretch every span below proportionally, but the pause beats stay full days at every cadence. They are not slack to compress out.

The course-day numbers below are calendar days from cohort start (Day 1 = content opens).

## Notes for the operator filling this in

1. **The pause beats are load-bearing.** The consolidation day after Module 6 and the rest day after Module 8 cushion the only stretches where High modules stack. Cohorts that compress them are the cohorts that lose people silently in week three.
2. **High modules do not unlock on mismatched pairings.** If partners drift more than one module apart for 4+ days, the witness-bench protocol runs (Doc 04 §I, [Witness Bench Onboarding](Witness%20Bench%20Onboarding.md)) before the calendar resumes for that learner.
3. **One calendar per learner is the ideal.** Learners on stretched cadence get their own filled copy; the cohort-level dates (calls, close, touchpoints) stay shared.
4. **Every ⚑ goes into the CM's own calendar with a reminder**, not just this document. The Run-Book's standing weekly loop (Part 2) covers everything between flags.
