# Cohort Feed Templates

| | |
|---|---|
| **For** | Community Manager (CM) running the cohort feed of an async Expand the Box cohort |
| **When** | Open this file before the cohort opens; use it weekly through closure |
| **Reads alongside** | `02 - Async Delivery Framework.md` Section D · `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` Sections F, H |

The cohort feed is **narrow on purpose.** Not a discussion board, not a support group, not a place for theory debate or long therapeutic confessionals: those have other homes (partner exchange, referral list, separate PM communities). The feed's one job: each learner posts a small specific signal (*I am still in the container, here is one thing alive in me*) and reads other learners' signals to know the cohort is alongside them.

The CM's job is to seed prompts that produce that kind of post, redirect drift toward it, and triage what shows up. Doc 04 holds the triage rubric and platform privacy minimums. This file holds the prompts and the language.

---

## 1. Cohort feed welcome post

The CM pins this at the top of the feed the day the cohort opens, before any module prompts go up. Verbatim:

```
Welcome. This is the cohort feed.

FOR:
  - One line of what landed in the module you just finished.
  - One line of what is sticking sideways.
  - Optional: one question for the group.
  - Optional: a witness offer — "if anyone needs a third party to 
    listen to something this week, I have 20 min Thursday."

NOT FOR:
  - Long processing of personal material. That goes to your partner.
  - Other people's stories.
  - Advice to other learners. The course teaches witnessing, not 
    advising. Resist the urge.
  - PM concept debate. Different forum — ask me and I'll point you.

RULES:
  - I read every post within 48h.
  - I classify in my head: GREEN (normal, no action), YELLOW (check-
    in DM within 24h), RED (active crisis — same-day response with 
    crisis line numbers in my first message).
  - Confidentiality holds; what's in the feed stays in the cohort.
  - You can delete your own posts; you can ask me to hide a post.

If a post feels too big for the feed, it probably is. Send it to 
your partner. If too big even for your partner, send it to me or 
use the referral list. The partner agreement applies here too — 
same confidentiality, same not-advising stance, same immediate-
danger protocol.

One line is enough. Often the smaller the post, the more it lands.

— [CM name]
```

## 2. Per-module cohort feed prompts

The CM posts one prompt to the feed at the start of each module. One or two sentences. The prompt asks for a small specific signal, never a story. Each prompt is voice-message-ready; the CM can post it as text, audio, or both.

**Day 1: Orientation, New Context, Radical Responsibility.**

> What sentence did you choose in the red-pill ceremony? Post the sentence (not the story).

**Day 2: Thoughtware, Thoughtmaps, Box Technology.**

> Name one move your Box made this week that you caught in the act. One sentence. (Not the story of the situation — the move itself.)

**Day 3: Liquid State, Center-Ground-Bubble, Five Bodies.**

> Which of the five bodies is loudest in you today, and which is quietest? Two words: loud body / quiet body.

**Day 4: Feedback, Coaching, Rapid Learning, Experiments.**

> What is one piece of feedback you received from your partner that landed somewhere unexpected? One sentence about what it landed in (the body location or the part of life). Not what they said.

**Day 5: Feelings vs Emotions, Old Map of Feelings, Numbness Bar.**

> Name one feeling you noticed under your Numbness Bar this module. One word.

**Day 6: Mixed Emotions and Emotional Healing Process.**

> Two-feeling combination you noticed in yourself this module. Format: "[feeling] + [feeling]." No story.

**Day 7: Low Drama, Gremlin Food, Shifting to Responsible Game.**

> One Low Drama role you caught yourself playing this week — Victim, Persecutor, or Rescuer. One sentence on the move, not the situation. (And: did the gremlin enjoy it?)

**Day 8: Listening, Speaking, Communication, Completion Loops.**

> Of the four Listenings, which one was hardest to hold in your Day 8 partner exchange? One word: defensive / verbatim / listening / possibility.

**Day 9: Ego States, Problem Ownership, Learning Spiral.**

> Which ego state surfaced for you most clearly in this module's work? One word. (And: did you let it speak, or did you collapse back to a different state?)

**Day 10: Map of Possibility, Bright Principles, Three Powers, Integration.**

> One declaration you are taking forward. (Not an intention; a declaration.)

## 3. What posts look like (good)

Examples the CM can hold as the shape. These are not posted as templates; they are reference for the CM's eye when triaging.

> **Day 1.** "Red pill. I am in." First time I have said something to myself I knew I meant.

> **Day 5.** Anger. Located in my jaw. I had been calling it tiredness for about six years.

> **Day 7.** Rescuer. The move was over-explaining in a work meeting so a colleague would not have to think. Gremlin loved it.

> **Day 10.** "I am the author of what my mornings become." Said it out loud to the room. Then again to my partner. Holding.

These posts share three traits: short, specific, and bring the signal back to *the learner*, not to the situation, not to other people, not to a theory.

## 4. What posts look like (drift)

Posts that need redirect, with the CM's verbatim DM language for each. The CM redirects privately, and may hide or delete the original depending on severity.

**Drift 1: long therapeutic confessional.** *"My mother always told me… [600 words]… and last night my husband again did the thing where…"*

```
Hi [name] — what you posted is real material; it belongs in your 
partner exchange, not the feed. I'll hide the post (text preserved 
— I'll send it back privately if you want it). Take it to [partner] 
as your next exchange. If it's bigger than the pairing can hold, 
use the referral list.
```

**Drift 2: advice to another learner.** *[Reply.] "I had something similar! What worked for me was…"*

```
Hi [name] — I'm hiding your reply. Not ill-intended — the feed 
isn't a place for advice between learners. The course teaches 
witnessing, not advising. What you do with the impulse: name what 
you noticed in yourself while reading their post, and say only 
that. If you want to offer a third-party witness call, the welcome 
post has the format.
```

**Drift 3: theory debate.** *"I'm not sure I agree with the four-levels frame — in my experience the boundary between adolescent and adult is more about… [400 words]"*

```
Hi [name] — the feed isn't for theory debate. The content is fixed; 
arguing it as a cohort turns the feed into a study group, a 
different gameworld. Here is the PM community where that lives: 
[link]. I'll hide your post; repost it there if you like.

Different question: is the thing you're arguing with conceptually 
hard to sit with as practice? If yes, partner exchange.
```

**Drift 4: witness offer that's actually advice in disguise.** *"If anyone is stuck on Day 5, I have a great technique I learned in another course I'd share over a call."*

```
Hi [name] — generous instinct. But "I'll share a technique" is 
teaching, not witnessing. A witness offer is: "if you need someone 
to listen to something for 20 min this week, I have Thursday." No 
technique, no teaching, no fixing. Repost in that format?
```

## 5. Cohort feed triage decision log

The CM keeps a short internal log of every post and DM, classified per the triage rubric in `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` Section F. The log protects the learner and the CM. It logs metadata only (the action taken), never the content of the post. Brief is fine.

Template per row:

| Date | Learner ID | Module | Post type | Flag | CM action | Follow-up date |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2026-02-12 | L-04 | Day 5 | Feed post | Green | None | — |
| 2026-02-12 | L-09 | Day 5 | Feed post | Yellow (stuck feeling >24h, named) | DM sent, offered voice call | 2026-02-13 |
| 2026-02-13 | L-09 | Day 5 | DM reply | Yellow (still stuck, decline call, accept stretched cadence) | Cadence adjusted, partner informed | 2026-02-15 |
| 2026-02-13 | L-11 | Day 5 | Feed post | Red (suicidal ideation, no plan named) | Crisis line numbers sent, clinician referral, cohort paused | 2026-02-14 |

The log lives in the same private spreadsheet as the pairing tracker (Doc 04 Section C), accessible only to the CM and their clinical supervisor. End-of-cohort review (Doc 04 Section L) pulls from this log.

## 6. Mid-course "what's alive" check-in prompt

Between Day 5 and Day 6 (after the first High module, before the most demanding one), a softer prompt that lets learners register where they are without producing material. Posted to the feed.

```
Mid-course check-in. Not a module prompt — a pause prompt.

Pick one. Post one. One sentence max.

  - The thing I came in looking for is _______ closer / further 
    away / different than I thought.
  - The body part loudest right now is _______.
  - The pace I am on is _______ right / too fast / too slow.
  - What I most want my partner to know about this week is _______.

No need to explain. I am reading.
```

The CM reads these within 24h (tighter than the normal 48h, because of mid-course intensity). Any "too fast" triggers a cadence DM. Any partner-related answer triggers a pairing-tracker check.

## 7. End-of-cohort gratitude prompts

After Day 10. Two posts the CM seeds.

**Partner-to-partner (learners reply tagging their partner).**

```
Pairings closed today. Cohort closes this week.

If you want to thank your partner publicly, this is the post. One 
or two sentences. Tag them. Specific is better than generic — what 
you carry forward FROM them, not "thanks for being amazing."

Thanking them in private only is also clean. The public version 
lets the cohort witness that the container held.
```

**Partner-to-cohort (CM seeds the next day, learners reply).**

```
Last post on this feed.

If you want to say one thing to the cohort — not to a person, to 
the room — say it here. The room held you while you held your 
partner while your partner held you. That circulation is the 
cohort.

One line. What you take with you. What you are leaving here.

Feed closes for posting [date, 7 days out], archived read-only for 
[duration]. Pairing continuation (if any) is between you and your 
partner; the cohort as a structure ends here.

Thank you for being in this container.

— [CM name]
```

After the gratitude window, the CM runs the post-cohort review (Doc 04 Section L).

---

The feed is small on purpose. Twelve good one-line posts per module is healthy. Three long posts per module is the feed pulling toward the wrong attractor: fix the prompts, fix the redirects, fix the welcome post if drift is persistent. Signal-to-noise on the feed is the CM's clearest read on whether the cohort is using the pairing or substituting the feed for it.
