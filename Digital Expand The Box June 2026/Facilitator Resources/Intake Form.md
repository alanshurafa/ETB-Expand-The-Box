# Intake Form

*The deployable enrollment intake. Part 1 is the form the prospective learner completes; copy-paste it into your platform as-is. Part 2 is the operator-only scoring and routing key; it never ships to learners. Thresholds and actions follow Doc 04 (Container and Gatekeeping Protocol) §A exactly.*

---

## Part 1: The intake form (learner-facing)

> **Before you start.** This form takes about 12 minutes and is completed before course access opens. Some questions are personal. Answering "yes" (or "I'd rather not say") routes you to a 1:1 conversation with the Community Manager, **not to automatic denial**. The screens exist to route you to the right support, including the support of a slower cadence or a later start. Honest answers are how the course keeps you safe inside it.

### A. About you

- Name: ______________ Preferred name: ______________
- Email (for cohort communications): ______________
- Country and state/province (used to build your region-specific support packet): ______________
- Time zone: ______________ Weekly availability windows (for partner matching): ______________
- Primary language for partner exchange: ______________

- Are you currently working with a therapist, coach, or psychiatrist? [ ] yes [ ] no *(just whether one exists, not a name)*
- Do you have someone (a friend, family member, or professional) you would call if you were in distress? [ ] yes [ ] no (optional: who, a word rather than a name): ______________
- **In the past 60 days,** have you lost someone close to you, or has a central relationship ruptured (separation, divorce, estrangement, major betrayal)? [ ] yes [ ] no
  *(If yes: the CM talks with you before access opens. The course's emotional-body modules land differently inside fresh grief or rupture; the usual adjustment is a slower cadence, not a closed door.)*

### B. Five screening questions

**B1.** In the past 30 days, have any of the following been true for you? *(tick all that apply)*

- [ ] I wished I were dead, or wished I could go to sleep and not wake up
- [ ] I had thoughts about killing myself
- [ ] I made a plan
- [ ] I intended to act on it
- [ ] None of the above

**B2.** In the past 6 months, have you experienced any of the following: flashbacks, lost time, feeling outside your body, inability to function after emotional work, intrusive memories that disrupted your day?

- [ ] Yes — monthly or more often, OR any lost time, OR any hospitalization for these
- [ ] Yes — less often than monthly, no lost time, and I am currently in care
- [ ] None of the above

**B3.** In the past 90 days, have any of the following felt out of your control to a degree that is affecting your life: alcohol, drugs (including cannabis), self-harm, restricting/bingeing food, gambling, sexual behavior, screens/scrolling?

- [ ] Yes — currently out of control, or I am less than 90 days from a severe addiction
- [ ] Compulsive but not out of control, and I am in active recovery support
- [ ] None of the above

**B4.** Is there anyone in your household, intimate relationship, workplace, visa status, custody arrangement, or financial life where speaking directly, setting a boundary, or shifting how you behave could put your safety, housing, children, or income at risk?

- [ ] Yes
- [ ] No

**B5.** In the past 12 months, have you experienced any of the following: manic episodes, psychosis, hearing or seeing things others did not, severe paranoia, psychiatric hospitalization, major changes to psychiatric medication?

- [ ] Yes, within the past 12 months
- [ ] Recovered — no episodes in the past 12 months, stable on medication, in care
- [ ] None of the above

## Part 2: Scoring and routing key (OPERATOR ONLY; never ships to learners)

**Routing principle (binding):** the screens route; they do not deny by default. Every flag routes to a named action (a CM 1:1, a support requirement, a cadence change, a defer-with-path), and the learner is told what the path back is. The one hard gate is B1's top tier, and even it carries a re-application path.

| Item | Response | Action (per Doc 04 §A) |
|---|---|---|
| **Age** | "18 or older" unconfirmed | No enrollment. The course is built for adults; there is no routed alternative. |
| **B1 Suicidality** | Plan, intent, attempt, or active ideation (boxes 2–4) | **No course access at this time.** Send crisis-line numbers + the licensed-clinician portion of the referral list. Offer the re-application path verbatim: *"the course will be available to you when a clinician confirms you are stable enough for thoughtware work."* |
| | Passive ideation only (box 1: wished not to wake, no plan) | **CM 1:1 consult required before access.** Confirm clinician or coach support exists. High modules (5, 6, 7, 9) require explicit confirmation from that support person before unlock. |
| | None | Continue. |
| **B2 Trauma & dissociation** | Tier 1 (monthly+, lost time, or hospitalization) | **Defer enrollment unless a trauma-qualified clinician (TF-CBT, EMDR, somatic, or equivalent) confirms participation in writing.** Stretched cadence. High modules require the same written confirmation. |
| | Tier 2 (less frequent, no lost time, in care) | CM 1:1 consult; proceed, stretched cadence recommended. |
| | None | Continue. |
| **B3 Addiction / compulsion** | Tier 1 (uncontrolled, or <90 days from severe addiction) | **Defer, or require active professional support in writing** (12-step sponsor + meeting attendance, or treating clinician). No High modules without an explicit written support plan. |
| | Tier 2 (compulsive, in active recovery support) | Continue, with CM 1:1 consult. |
| | None | Continue. |
| **B4 Coercive / unsafe environment** | Yes | **Access proceeds.** No direct-confrontation experiments involving that person. Provide DV + legal resources (Referral List §6) at enrollment; substitute experiments with the lower-stakes variants now marked under every item in the Experiment Bank. Tell the learner this is not because they cannot handle the work but because the course refuses to create risk in their actual life. **Confirm the referral-packet delivery channel is private before sending.** |
| | No | Continue. |
| **B5 Mania / psychosis** | Yes, past 12 months | **Defer access unless the prescribing clinician confirms participation in writing.** Archetypal and ego-state material (Module 9 especially) can amplify these states. |
| | Recovered, stable, in care | CM 1:1 consult; proceed. |
| | None | Continue. |
| **60-day bereavement / rupture** | Yes | CM consult before access opens. **Default adjustment: stretched cadence**, with the emotional-body modules (5–6) explicitly discussed in the consult. A closed door is not the default. |
| **C self-check** | Two or more "no" | CM 1:1 before access; route per what surfaces (often overlaps a B-screen flag). |
| **A: no distress contact** | "No" on the distress-contact question | Not a gate. Flag for the CM: this learner's referral packet and partner pairing carry more load; confirm packet delivery and partner match before access. |

**Order of operations.** 1) Apply screens. 2) Resolve every flagged route (consults held, written confirmations on file). 3) Generate and deliver the per-learner referral packet (Referral List). 4) Confirm acknowledgments. 5) Open access. A learner with an unresolved route has no content access — that is the gate, not a suggestion.

**Record-keeping.** Store the completed form and any written confirmations per your platform-privacy minimums (Doc 04 §H). The CM never posts or discusses screen results in any shared space; routing conversations are 1:1 only.
