# M40 — EHP Learnings

*A second Go!/Beep! card, harvested from many EHPs — the client's discipline as much as the spaceholder's, and the one line worth keeping for any solo rep: thinking over feeling is the Beep!*

![EHP Learnings — Possibility Management map](../Maps/M40.png)

**What it is.** A second Go!/Beep! card, harvested from many Emotional Healing Processes, describing the client's discipline as much as the spaceholder's. The Go! column: the feeling is found in the body; the body leads and the words follow; the client gets space to experience rather than narrate; the voice stays even and strong; both people are on one team; the emotion is sensed where it actually sits, right at the surface. The Beep! column is a catalog of the ways the process gets stolen: performing instead of feeling; distractions; telling the client what they feel; firing too many questions too fast; the verdict "you are doing this"; coaching when the moment asked for witnessing; thinking over feeling; and managing the client toward a goal, the subtle one, because a goal-shaped EHP is no longer a discovery. If you keep one line, keep thinking over feeling: every learner hits the moment where the mind offers a tidy explanation in exchange for not feeling the next wave.

**At a glance.** Go! → the feeling found in the body · body leads, words follow · space to experience, not narrate · voice even and strong · one team · emotion sensed at the surface · Beep! → performing instead of feeling · telling the client what they feel · too many questions too fast · coaching where witnessing was asked · thinking over feeling · steering toward a goal · A goal-shaped EHP is managed, not discovered → what moves, and how far, is not schedulable · Crying or shaking is not the marker → the feeling moving in the body and the return to present is · **Study-level only here** → the doing lives behind the gate.

## How to explain it verbally

This is the second Go!/Beep! card from the healing work, and it is about the client's discipline as much as the witness's. The Go! column: the feeling gets found in the body, the body leads and the words follow, the client gets space to experience instead of narrate, the voice stays even and strong, both people are on one team. The Beep! column catalogs how the process gets stolen: performing instead of feeling, telling the client what they feel, firing off too many questions, coaching when the moment asked for plain witnessing, and steering toward a goal, which quietly turns a discovery into a managed exercise. If you keep one line from this whole map, keep "thinking over feeling." Every single person hits the moment where the mind offers a tidy explanation in exchange for not having to feel the next wave. That explanation is the Beep!

**If you only remember one thing:** thinking over feeling is the classic Beep! of the healing work; the tidy explanation is the mind buying its way out of the next wave.
