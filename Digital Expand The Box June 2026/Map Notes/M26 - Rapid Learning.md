# M26 — Rapid Learning

*The fast inner loop by which a person upgrades thoughtware in ordinary life — Go! → Beep! → Shift! → Go! — and the one swamp that kills it.*

![Map of Rapid Learning — Possibility Management map](../Maps/M26.png)

**What it is.** The map is a loop, not a line: three stations on a wheel that keeps turning. **GO!** — you act, you try the thing. **BEEP!** — something did not work as planned. **SHIFT!** — you change one specific variable and run again, the arrow back to GO! being the do-over that is the whole point. The BEEP! box carries the correction — *This is NOT a failure* — because a Beep! is design data, not a verdict. Both Go! and Beep! are feedback. The loop only dies when Beep! collapses into "I am bad" and the actor slides off the wheel into the **Feedback Swamp**.

**At a glance.** Loop → Go! → Beep! → Shift! → Go! (the last arrow is the do-over) · Go! = worked · Beep! = didn't work as planned — both are feedback · A Beep! is design data, not a verdict on your worth · A Shift! is one specific variable, not your whole identity · Feedback Swamp → "oh no / I can't do anything right / they're attacking me" — the wheel jams · Exit → "Thank you for the feedback," log it in the Beep! Book, name the Shift!, Go! again.
