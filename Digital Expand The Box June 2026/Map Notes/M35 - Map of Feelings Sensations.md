# M35 — Map of Feelings Sensations

*The body-sensation lookup table for the four feelings, and the answer to "how do I know which feeling this is?" — because the sensation is the feeling and the thought about it is the story.*

![Map of Feelings Sensations — Possibility Management map](../Maps/M35.png)

**What it is.** Four quadrants, one per feeling, and not a single abstraction in any of them. No "I felt disrespected," no "it triggered me." Just heat, racing heart, lump in the throat, cold belly, tingling eyes, shiver, bright eyes, pressing lips. This map answers the question every learner asks in their first feelings practice: how do I know which feeling this is? You know by sensation. Anger announces itself as heat and rigidity pressing outward; sadness as melting, chest-deep heaviness and a thickening throat; fear as electricity, shiver and racing thoughts and a cold belly; joy as opening, lightness and warmth that starts in the eyes. Used as a two-way lookup table, it is the map you are actually running every morning in the bar reading.

**At a glance.** The sensation is the feeling; the thought about it is the story · Anger → heat, rigidity, energy pressing outward · Sadness → melting, heaviness, thickening throat, slow inward turn · Fear → shiver, contraction, racing thoughts, cold or fluttering belly · Joy → opening, lightness, warmth starting in the eyes · Two-way lookup → name a feeling and verify it in the body, or take an unnamed sensation and find which quadrant you are in · The map lists common signatures, not an exhaustive catalog → your feeling may have its own dialect.

## How to explain it verbally

Every learner asks the same question in their first feelings practice: how do I actually know which feeling this is? This map is the answer, and the answer is sensation, not thought. The sensation is the feeling; the thought about it is the story. Anger shows up as heat and rigidity, energy pressing outward. Sadness is melting, a heaviness in the chest, a thickening throat. Fear is electricity, a shiver, a cold or fluttering belly. Joy is opening, lightness, warmth that starts in the eyes. You use it both directions. Sometimes you have named a feeling and you check it against the body. More often you have an unnamed sensation, a tight throat or a cold belly, and the map tells you which quadrant you are standing in.

**If you only remember one thing:** you identify a feeling by its body sensation, not by the story about it, and this map is your two-way lookup.
