# M33 — Feelings vs Emotions

*The two-panel distinction the whole emotional-body work hangs on — feelings originate now and last minutes; emotions originate in the past and last longer; feelings are for creating, emotions are for healing.*

![Feelings vs Emotions — Possibility Management map](../Maps/M33.png)

**What it is.** Two panels, top and bottom, with the same four names in each: anger, sadness, fear, joy. The top panel says feelings originate in the present and last three to five minutes at most; the bottom panel says emotions originate in the past, last longer than five minutes, and arrive mixed. Each panel ends by naming what that class is *for*: feelings are for creating, in the now; emotions are for healing. The popular world uses the two words interchangeably; PM does not. A feeling is present-time, clean, informational, and short. An emotion is a feeling that was not allowed to move, got stored, and now runs on autopilot, often triggered by something in the present that resembles the past.

**At a glance.** Feeling → originates now · 3–5 min · clean · informational · for creating · Emotion → originates in the past · longer than 5 min · mixed · story-laden · for healing · The clearest field test → the 5-minute test: still happening after five minutes means you are in an emotion · The trap → mistaking a stored emotion for a present feeling and "expressing" it at the person who only triggered it · An emotion often carries the voice of the external authority that blocked the feeling the first time · Neither class is bad → an emotion is a gateway to the healing work, not a defect.

## How to explain it verbally

Picture two panels with the same four feelings in each. The top panel is feelings: they start now, in the present, and last three to five minutes, then pass. They are clean and informational. The bottom panel is emotions: they start in the past, last longer than five minutes, and arrive mixed and story-laden. The whole module hangs on this split, and the sentence that closes each panel matters: feelings are for creating, emotions are for healing. The clearest field test is just the clock. If five minutes later you are still in it, you are in an emotion, not a present feeling. The classic mistake is feeling an old stored anger, mistaking it for present information, and expressing it at a partner who was only the trigger, never the source.

**If you only remember one thing:** feelings start now and pass in minutes; emotions are stored from the past and loop, and the clock is how you tell them apart.
