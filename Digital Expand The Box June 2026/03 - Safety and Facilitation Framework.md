# 03 — Safety and Facilitation Framework

> This document is the operational safety baseline for Expand the Box delivered async online.
> A community manager (CM) reads it cover-to-cover before opening a cohort.
> A learner reads sections **B**, **C**, **D**, **F** and **I** before starting Module 1 (Module 00 assembles them in learner voice).
> Section letters A–M match v1 so existing cross-references hold; v2 additions are sections N–Q and the marked upgrades inside A, H and I.

---

## A. What this course is not

**Expand the Box is not:**
- Therapy
- Counseling
- Trauma treatment
- Medical or psychiatric care
- A substitute for any of the above

It is an educational course in the thoughtware of Possibility Management. It will bring into view: Box survival strategies the learner has been identified with, feelings that have been unfelt, numbness in places that used to be alive, Low Drama payoffs the learner has been collecting, old decisions made under threat that are still running. That is by design. Discomfort is part of the work. Discomfort that overwhelms the learner is **not** part of the work and is the cue to pause and reach for professional support.

### The pre-course self-screen (plain language; v2 rewrite)

v1 phrased this screen in course vocabulary ("unable to center, ground, drop a bubble, return to Adult"), which a newcomer has not yet learned. The plain version a prospective learner can actually answer:

A person should **wait** to take this course, take it slowly with active professional support alongside, or take it later, if right now any of these is true:

- You are in a mental health crisis, or a clinician has told you that you are close to one.
- Someone close to you died within the last 60 days (case by case; see the intake question below).
- You are in the active middle of a major life rupture: a separation, job loss, eviction, or a serious new diagnosis.
- When something upsets you strongly, you usually cannot settle your body again within an hour or two, alone or with a friend; strong feelings tend to take you over for a day or more.
- Difficult material from your past is already in motion (in therapy or on its own) and you have no therapist, counselor, or coach currently alongside you.

The CM applies the **full intake screening protocol** in [04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md](04%20-%20Container%20and%20Gatekeeping%20Protocol.md) §A: five specific screens (suicidality, trauma and dissociation, addiction, coercive environment, mania/psychosis) with explicit thresholds and actions, plus the 18+ line and the bereavement question. The screening is the gate, not a recommendation.

### The bereavement / active-rupture screen (v2 addition: the 60-day rule made operational)

v1 stated the 60-day default here and in Module 6 but never asked at intake. v2 asks. The intake form carries one Y/N question (form wording lives in Doc 04 §A.1):

> *In the last 60 days, has someone close to you died, or has a major part of your life come apart (separation, job loss, eviction, serious diagnosis)?*

**Y →** a 1:1 CM consult before access, and the **stretched cadence as the default offer**, not an option the learner has to discover. The consult is routing, not denial: some recent losses coexist well with this work when support is in place; the CM checks that it is. **N →** continue.

## B. The four working agreements

A learner who begins Module 1 is agreeing to four things. They are repeated at the start of every module.

1. **Choice is always available.** The learner can pause, skip a practice, slow down, take a longer break between modules, or stop entirely. Nothing in the course is mandatory. No PM teacher anywhere requires a learner to "push through" anything. If the learner's body says no, the answer is no.
2. **Consent is ongoing.** The course asks the learner's consent at the start of each high-intensity practice. Consent given at enrollment does not carry forward into every exercise. The learner can withdraw consent for any single practice and continue with the course.
3. **The learner is responsible for their own state.** The course teaches the practices: center, ground, drop a bubble, feel, choose, ask, declare. The learner practices using them. A facilitator is not present in real time. If the learner cannot access the practices in a given moment, they pause and reach for support: partner, CM, or the referral list.
4. **The witness is alive.** Before starting Modules 5, 6, 7 and 9, the learner confirms their partner (or recruited witness) is reachable. If not, the module waits. This is not a suggestion. It is a structural requirement.

## C. Intensity tags

Every module is tagged Low / Medium / High. The tag is in the header block and drives pacing and check-in requirements.

| Tag | Modules | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Low** | 0, 1, 3, 4, 10, 11 | Largely cognitive and embodied-light. May surface feeling but is unlikely to put the learner in unfamiliar territory. No required partner check-in before the module. **Module 3 is Low *emotional* intensity but structurally load-bearing: it installs the center/ground/bubble/cube equipment every High module depends on. Do not skim it.** |
| **Medium** | 2, 8 | Module 2: disidentification + Box inventory + survival-layer preview. Module 8: direct relational practice on the learner's actual material; for many, as intense as the feelings work. **Partner check-in before starting is named in the module header; debrief after.** |
| **High** | 5, 6, 7, 9 | Directly engages the emotional body, drama patterns, ego states, or the Gremlin. May surface material the learner has not seen before. **Required partner check-in before; required debrief after. Gated by the unlock checklist in Doc 04 §D.** High-module headers also link the re-entry page (§O) and `ground.html`. |

There is **no skip-after-24-hours override** for the High-module check-in. The earlier framework permitted it; it is retracted. If a learner cannot meet the unlock checklist, they wait. The course will still be there in a week.

**Modules 5–9 are the climb** and the course says so out loud: four High modules and a heavy Medium, cushioned by the integration pause after Module 6 and the rest day between Modules 8 and 9. The pause beats are part of the safety architecture, not scheduling advice.

## D. The universal grounding script

Available at the top of every module file as a callout box, and shipped standalone as **[`Interactive Tools/ground.html`](Interactive%20Tools/ground.html)**: bookmarkable, one tap from every gated tool's GROUND NOW button. Reproduced here:

> **Grounding (60 seconds).**
> 1. Stop whatever you were doing in the module. Close the video or the document.
> 2. Stand up. Both feet on the floor. Knees soft.
> 3. Breathe out longer than you breathe in, three times. Audible exhale is fine.
> 4. Name three things you can see in the room.
> 5. Name one thing you can hear.
> 6. Place one hand on your sternum, the other on your belly. Notice that your body is here.
> 7. When you have come back, decide: continue, pause for 10 minutes, or stop the module for today.

This script is the default action whenever a learner notices any of the following:
- Feeling far away from themselves
- Inability to follow the words on the page or in the video
- Heart racing without clear cause
- The sense that they are watching themselves from outside
- Crying that does not feel like a clean feeling but feels stuck
- Strong urge to flee, attack, or shut down

These are signals to ground first, then decide. They are not signals that something is wrong with the learner. They are signals that the nervous system needs a beat.

## E. When to pause the module

The course teaches a clean distinction:

- **Discomfort that is moving** → keep going gently. This is the work.
- **Discomfort that is stuck or escalating** → ground (Section D), then pause for at least 10 minutes, then re-decide.
- **Discomfort that is unmistakably crisis** → stop, contact the partner, and if needed the referral list (Section H).

The learner does not have to perfectly classify their state. The default action when uncertain is to ground and pause. The course losing 10 minutes to caution is fine. The learner pushing through a state that needed a pause is not.

## F. When to call the partner

The partner pairing is not just for the structured exchanges. It is also a real-time resource. A learner sends the partner a voice message — short, no production — in any of these situations:

- They notice they have been in a stuck feeling for more than 24 hours
- They notice they have been numbing (substances, screens, work, food) more than usual since the module
- They had a strong reaction during the module and want to be witnessed
- They are about to do something significant in their life that the module surfaced (a difficult conversation, leaving a job, ending a relationship) — partner is witness, not advisor
- They notice they are stalling on the next module and want to talk about why

Partners do not advise. Partners witness, reflect, and ask the questions taught in Modules 8 and 9. If the partner is over their head, **the partner contacts the CM**, not the learner's professional support. The CM decides whether to escalate.

### Partner immediate-danger protocol

> **If your partner tells you they may harm themselves or someone else, ask: "Are you in immediate danger right now?"**
>
> If **yes** — contact local emergency services or your country's crisis line AND the CM **immediately.** Do not wait for course protocol. Do not wait for the CM's 24-hour SLA. Stay with your partner in the message thread until they have connected with crisis services.
>
> If **no** — voice-message the CM the same day. The CM will reach out to your partner directly.

This rule is item 4 of the 7-line agreement every partner and every recruited witness signs (Doc 04 §B). A person who has not signed it is not eligible for the witness role. This section, the Module 6 witness scripts, and the **Partner Emergency Card** (Facilitator Resources safety pack) together form the witness annex a recruited witness reads before any High module (Doc 04 §B2). Solo readers without a witness route to the defer-list instead (§P).

## G. When to call the CM

The CM is reachable by direct message with a guaranteed reply within 24 hours **for routine messages.** For active crisis (red flag in the triage rubric — Doc 04 §F), the standard is **immediate on detection, with first response within 15 minutes** of the CM (or named backup) seeing the message, crisis line numbers in that first reply. The CM ensures a named backup with accepted role + platform access + crisis protocol covers any window when the CM is unreachable.

A learner reaches the CM when:

- They want to pause the cohort for personal reasons
- Their partner is unresponsive or the pairing is not working
- Something happened in their life since enrollment that affects whether they should continue
- They are concerned about another learner in the cohort
- They want a referral
- They are considering stopping the course (see §O; stopping well is a held path, and the CM holds it)

The CM does **not** provide coaching or therapy. The CM helps the learner make a structural decision and points to resources.

## H. The referral pathway

The template list lives in [Facilitator Resources/Referral List.md](Facilitator%20Resources/Referral%20List.md). The operational artifact is the **per-learner referral packet** generated from intake (Doc 04 §E) and confirmed before content access opens. Crisis lines are listed first, never buried. **France's line is 3114** (national, 24/7) in every artifact where crisis lines appear: the Referral List, the per-learner packets, and any tool or page that names lines.

**The CM refers when:**
- A learner expresses suicidal ideation, intent or plan → crisis line first, then licensed clinician
- A learner is processing material clearly beyond the course's scope (active trauma, abuse history, addiction) → licensed clinician
- A learner wants 1:1 PM-aware support beyond the partner pairing → PM-aware coach
- A learner is well and wants to deepen → PM Trainer Path or local PM community

**Referral language template (CM, when needed):**
> What I'm hearing is bigger than what the course is built to hold. That doesn't mean you're doing the work wrong — it means the work you're doing right now needs someone qualified to be with you in it. Here are three options: [coach / clinician / line]. The course will be here when you come back. Do you want me to put the cohort on pause for you?

## I. UNIFIED CONSENT SPEC (v2: one component, one behavior, everywhere)

v1 had the right consent stance and one hole (a tool that persisted consent forever) and one side door (an ungated Atlas EHP). v2 unifies consent into a single specification that every gated surface implements. The spec is binding on all current and future tools:

1. **Per-visit, never persisted.** The consent block re-arms on every page load. No consent state is ever written to localStorage, cookies, or anywhere else. There is no "remember my choice." Consent to survival-layer or High-intensity work is a present-tense act; yesterday's yes is data, not consent.
2. **Day-specific items.** Each gated surface names what *this* practice engages (feelings work, the EHP, drama patterns, ego states, the survival layer) and the day-specific confirmations, not a generic checkbox.
3. **Real gating (`inert`).** Until consent is given, the practice content is programmatically inert: not focusable, not readable by shortcut, not scrolled past. The gate is structural, not decorative.
4. **GROUND NOW always reachable from the gate.** The grounding script (§D / `ground.html`) is available *at* the consent screen, not only behind it. A learner deciding whether to consent may already need it.
5. **No skip-override.** There is no "I'll confirm later" path. Declining is a full, honored choice: the page offers the locator-level alternative or simply today's exit.

**The learner-facing consent prompt** (each High module's header, verbatim core):

> **Consent check.**
> This module engages [feelings / drama patterns / ego states / etc.] directly. Material that has been managed by avoidance may surface. You are choosing to do this.
>
> Before continuing, confirm to yourself:
> - My partner (or recruited witness) is reachable today and tomorrow.
> - I have at least 90 uninterrupted minutes ahead of me.
> - I am not in acute crisis or under the influence right now.
> - I know how to ground (see top of file).
> - I know how to reach the CM if I need to.
>
> If any of the above is not true, pause this module and come back when it is.
> If all of the above is true and you choose to continue, take a slow breath and begin.

**Implementing tools, named:** the Module 2 survival-layer shield in `Interactive Tools/Day 02/box-inventory.html`; the High-day gates in `Day 05/feeling-locator.html`, `Day 06/ehp-walker.html`, `Day 07/drama-detector.html`, and `Day 09/ego-state-locator.html`; and the Atlas: **`Map Atlas/M12` carries no live EHP walk-through; it describes the process and routes to the gated Day 6 walker.** Solo-reader variants of the Module 5/6/7/9 consent blocks carry the "Reading this without a cohort?" box (§P). Any new gated tool implements all five points or does not ship; the Manifest's no-consent-key rule (Manifest §6) is the regression check.

This is not bureaucratic. It is the consent moment that distinguishes a chosen practice from a thing happening to the learner.

## J. Special-population notes

- **Survivors of trauma.** PM is precise that *emotional healing process work* (in scope) is distinct from *trauma processing* (not in scope). Survivors should be in concurrent care with a trauma-qualified clinician and check with that clinician about whether to take the course now or later. The CM does not screen these clinically.
- **Learners on psychiatric medication.** PM does not contraindicate medication. The course does not advise changing medication. Any medication question goes to the prescribing physician.
- **Neurodivergent learners.** The course's pacing assumes the learner can self-regulate length-of-engagement and break frequency; the sitting markers help, and the stretched cadence (Doc 02 §K) is freely available. The CM asks, not assumes.
- **Learners in current grief.** Recent loss + this course can be productive or destabilizing depending on the loss, the time elapsed, and the support system. Default: wait at least 60 days post-loss and have a therapist or grief-aware coach alongside. The intake question in §A makes this operational instead of aspirational.

## K. CM safety practices (for the CM, not the learner)

- **Read every cohort-feed post within 48 hours.** No exceptions. (24 hours during High windows.)
- **Log direct-message exchanges** (date, learner, summary, action taken). Brief is fine. The log protects the learner and the CM.
- **Keep the referral list current.** Quarterly check that listed clinicians and coaches are still practicing and accepting clients.
- **Refer without flinching.** A CM who delays referral because they want to "help first" is contributing to harm.
- **Hold confidentiality.** Cohort-feed posts are not shared outside the cohort. Direct-message exchanges are not shared with the learner's partner or other learners. The only exceptions: imminent risk of harm, legal compulsion, or the learner's own explicit consent.
- **Have a CM-side check-in.** The CM should themselves have a supervisor, peer, or coach to debrief with. Holding a cohort is real work.

## L. Incident response (if something goes wrong)

If a learner experiences a serious adverse event during or attributable to the course (psychiatric crisis, hospitalization, significant rupture):

1. **CM contacts the learner** — immediately if active crisis (red flag); within 24 hours otherwise. Plain language. Asks what happened, what they need, whether they are safe right now.
2. **CM offers** pause, refund (safety-routed exits always refund in full, Doc 05 Step 0), referral, or continued cohort access. The learner chooses.
3. **CM documents** the incident factually and confidentially.
4. **CM debriefs** with their own supervisor or peer.
5. **CM reviews** the course content the learner had been engaging with. If a pattern emerges across multiple learners with the same module, the module is revised before the next cohort.

This is not a defensive posture. It is the minimum honest response to material that engages people's inner lives.

## M. Three things this document does not pretend

1. **It does not pretend the course is safe in all cases.** It is safe for most adults who self-select into a thoughtware course and engage with reasonable care. It is not appropriate for everyone. The screening, the pacing controls and the referral pathway exist because of this.
2. **It does not pretend the CM is a therapist.** A CM holding a cohort with this framework is doing real and skilled work, but it is the work of a community caretaker, not a clinician.
3. **It does not pretend that doing nothing is also safe.** Many of the learners drawn to this course are already in pain — pain that the absence of PM-grade thoughtware was not preventing. The decision to take the course (or to recommend it) is always weighed against the alternative of continuing without it.

## N. The next-morning check after every High module (v2 addition)

The morning after completing Module 5, 6, 7 or 9, before the daily sit and before opening anything new, the learner answers three questions in the Beep! Book. One line each:

1. **Body:** *Where is my body this morning: settled, tender, or still charged?*
2. **Overnight:** *Did yesterday's material keep moving in the night (dreams, looping thoughts, waking numbness)?*
3. **Need:** *What do I need before any new content: an ordinary day, a partner message, or a CM check-in?*

Routing is built into the answers: *settled / no / ordinary day* → proceed normally. *Tender or charged*, or *yes* on overnight → run the grounding script, take a no-new-content day, and send the partner one line. Anything still charged at the **second** morning → message the CM (that is the §F "stuck more than 24 hours" signal, caught early). The check takes under two minutes and is listed in each High module's flow close, after Script 9.

## O. Exit ramps and re-entry (v2 addition)

**Stopping well is a held path, not a failure mode.** The course says this to learners and equips the CM to hold it:

- **Learner-facing:** Module 11 carries the "stopping well" half-page: how to close the partner exchange cleanly, what to keep (the spine sit, the Beep! Book), how to leave the door open. It applies whether the learner stops at Module 4 or finishes everything.
- **CM-facing:** the **CM Leaver Checklist** (Facilitator Resources safety pack) sequences a clean exit: closure exchange offered, partner re-matched or released, access and refund handled per Doc 05 Step 0, referral packet re-sent, and a **leaver wellbeing check** (one scheduled CM message ~2 weeks after the exit, no reply required, logged metadata-only).
- **Re-entry:** [`Practice/Coming Back.md`](Practice/Coming%20Back.md) is the learner's return path (3+/7+/30+ day tiers, anti-binge rule, the return framed as a rep). It is linked from `START HERE.md` and from **every High-module header**, because the days after a High module are where silent absence most often starts.

## P. Solo-reader policy (v2 addition)

Per ARCHITECTURE decision 3 and Doc 02 §G: solo readers are sanctioned, the gates are intact. Safety-relevant specifics:

- Low and Medium modules are fully open solo. The High modules require a witness: either **recruit-a-witness** (a friend qualifies; protocol and disqualifiers in Doc 04 §B2; the witness reads §F's annex, the Module 6 witness script, and the Partner Emergency Card) or the **defer-list** (read the teaching, run locator-level practices, defer the EHP and Demon-adjacent work until witnessed).
- Solo readers have no CM and no SLA. The "Reading this without a cohort?" boxes say so plainly and name [findahelpline.com](https://findahelpline.com) as the referral floor.
- The defer-list is a boundary, not a workaround. A solo reader who self-runs the EHP against it is outside the course's design, and the course says that in advance rather than discovering it after.

## Q. The Screen-4 variant system (v2 addition)

Doc 04's Screen 4 (coercive or unsafe environment) promises low-stakes experiment substitutions; v2 builds them. **Every between-module experiment in the [Experiment Bank](Facilitator%20Resources/Experiment%20Bank.md) carries a lower-stakes variant column**: same distinction trained, no direct confrontation with the flagged person, no action that could touch safety, housing, children, or income.

Modules 1, 7, 8 and 10 (the modules whose default experiments are most confrontation-shaped) carry a **discreet learner-facing cue**, one line, no flag visible to anyone else: *"If a direct version of this experiment could put your safety, housing, or income at risk, run the variant column version. That is the course working, not you opting out."* The CM substitutes variants silently for Screen-4-flagged learners at experiment touchpoints; the learner's record shows nothing in the feed.
