# 01 — Course Architecture and Pedagogy

## What this course is

**Expand the Box** is a twelve-module (00–11), self-paced online course that teaches the core thoughtware of **Possibility Management (PM)**: the body of distinctions, maps and practices developed by Clinton Callahan and the PM community since the late 1980s.

It is a **thoughtware upgrade**. The learner's Box, the survival-and-protection thoughtware they have been identified with, becomes visible enough that they can disidentify from it, enter liquid state, choose a new context, and run experiments from there. The course is not "self-improvement"; it is an apprenticeship in catching the Box in the act and choosing differently while it is happening.

It is **not** therapy, coaching, or a workshop of prepackaged techniques. It is an apprenticeship in noticing, supported by maps, distinctions, embodied practices, partner pairings, one evolving daily practice, and structured between-module experiments. v2's framing claim: this is a **practice container, not a content library**. Every architectural choice below serves that.

## The pedagogical premise

PM rests on a single load-bearing claim:

> What you are able to think with determines what becomes possible to choose, feel, speak and create. Upgrading thoughtware is therefore upstream of any change in behavior, relationship, or what life can become.

Everything follows from that:

1. **Maps before practice.** Each module installs the relevant map first, then asks the learner to live inside it. A learner who tries to "do anger differently" without a new map of feelings reproduces the old map's habits in different costume.
2. **Distinctions before techniques.** PM gives **distinctions** the learner uses, not techniques to master. A distinction is a perceptual cut; once seen, it changes what is available. Examples: *feeling vs emotion*, *Box vs you*, *low drama vs responsible game*, *is-glue vs has-glue*.
3. **Five-body learning.** Cognitive content alone reinstalls the Box. Each module engages at least three of the five bodies (intellectual, emotional, physical, energetic, archetypal) so the learning lands where new behavior originates.
4. **Practice in the small.** New choices stabilize through small experiments: a single sentence said in a single conversation. Not a transformation. A rep. The reps compound.
5. **No authority transfer.** Prompts ask the learner to verify each distinction in their own direct experience, not to take the writer's word.

## Key distinctions baked into the architecture

Seven distinctions sit underneath every module. They carry forward from v1 unchanged in substance; canonical wordings live in the [Voice and Style Guide](Facilitator%20Resources/Voice%20and%20Style%20Guide.md) ledger, which wins any wording dispute.

1. **Context (the PM definition).** The invisible source-frame that determines what actions, meanings, choices, and results are even available. Upstream of mindset and beliefs. The course works on context; most learners arrive trying to change behavior.
2. **Possibility paradigm vs causal paradigm.** The causal paradigm explains the present from the past; the possibility paradigm creates from a chosen context. The course does not ask the learner to abandon causal explanation, only to spend twelve modules testing context as the more leveraged frame.
3. **Gameworld.** The course is a gameworld the learner enters by choice: explicit rules (the four agreements), roles (learner · partner/witness · CM · CM's supervisor), agreements, consequences, a duration, and a boundary. Naming it tells the learner they are entering an explicit, finite container, not joining anything.
4. **Archiarchy vs patriarchy.** PM points toward archiarchy: culture generated by sourcing bright principles. Patriarchy, the cultural matrix most learners were raised inside, is generated by sourcing shadow principles. (Causation direction per ledger CORRECTION 2: principle upstream, culture downstream.) The course asks for experiments, not identity adoption.
5. **Box layers.** Surface (behaviors), story (beliefs and narratives), survival (identity-level commitments formed under threat). The course paces these: Module 2 stays mostly at surface and story; Modules 5–6 touch survival material through the emotional body; Module 9 gives the tools to work it more safely.
6. **Awareness vs consciousness.** Awareness notices; consciousness holds distinctions and chooses from them. PM is a consciousness training. Mindfulness builds awareness; PM uses awareness to install distinctions, then uses distinctions to enable choice.
7. **Being vs Box vs Gremlin.** Three locations to speak, feel, and choose from. The learner has a Box and has a Gremlin and is neither. Module 9's five ego states only work if this distinction is already held.

One more architectural term, fixed by ledger CORRECTION 3: **matrix** means, everywhere by default, *the energetic structure you build through practice that determines how much consciousness you can hold*. Thoughtware upgrades install onto matrix. The cultural-matrix sense is always written with a qualifier; the old "structured field" delivery sense is retired in favor of "container."

## The learning arc (why this order)

The arc moves **container → frame → embodiment → emotional body → relational body → integration → continuation**, each module installing what the next assumes.

| Phase | Modules | What this phase installs |
|---|---|---|
| **Container** | 00 | The container itself: cohort, recruited witness, or solo path · Beep! Book · the schedule |
| **Frame** | 1, 2 | New context · the chain · radical responsibility · the Box as filter |
| **Embodiment infrastructure** | 3, 4 | Liquid state · center/ground/bubble · five bodies · feedback · experiments |
| **Emotional body** | 5, 6 | Feelings vs emotions · old map · Numbness Bar · mixed emotions · EHP |
| **Relational body** | 7, 8 | Low drama and gremlin food · the Responsible Game · listening · speaking · completion loops |
| **Integration** | 9, 10 | Ego states · problem ownership · learning spiral · Map of Possibility · bright/shadow principles · three powers |
| **Continuation** | 11 | The 90-day container · Possibility Team · stopping well · what the learner takes with them |

The v1 rationale holds: container before map (without the authored stance, maps become information to consume); embodiment before feelings (Module 3 installs the equipment Modules 5–6 use); emotional before relational (low drama is mechanically an avoidance of one's own feelings); ego states near the end (the integration lens).

**The climb is named.** Modules 5–9 stack four High modules and a heavy Medium. The architecture cushions it deliberately: the integration pause after Module 6 (at least one full low-demand day, with a 15-minute recall-based consolidation rep inside it), the corrected Module 7 framing (Modules 8 and 9 are still ahead), and a scheduled rest day between Modules 8 and 9. Module 00's printable schedule shows all of this before the learner starts.

## THE RETRIEVAL LINE (binding; contributors do not re-litigate this)

The course's no-quiz stance (Doc 02 §J) is load-bearing: PM is verified in lived experience, and the course rewards nothing. v2 adds a retrieval layer **inside** that stance. The line between the two is drawn here, once, for good:

**Generation: YES.** Any practice where the learner produces the material from their own memory and experience, and the course's only response is the learner's own next look at the map:

- **Free recall** — every module opens with a 5-minute warm-up: redraw or restate the prior module's central map from memory, then look at the map.
- **Teach-back** — the learner explains a map to their partner in their own words (Modules 3, 6, 9), seeded by the Map Notes' "How to explain it verbally" paragraphs.
- **Self-explanation** — "say why this step is here before reading on."
- **Perform-the-outcome** — show the move (run a completion loop, do a bar reading) rather than describe knowing it.
- **Unscored flip-card recall** — the Atlas flip-cards, framed as recall prompts: see the term, say the distinction out loud, flip. Nothing records whether the learner "got it."
- **Close-the-loop self-check** — each module ends with the existing three-word scale (the Learning Self-Assessment's *not yet / partly / landed*), self-applied, never collected per-name.

**Grading: NO. Forever.** Anything where the course (a tool, a page, a person in a course role) checks an answer and renders a verdict:

- Answer-checking of any kind. No tool compares the learner's recall to a stored correct answer.
- Scoring, points, percentages, streaks, progress bars framed as achievement.
- Right/wrong feedback, including softened forms ("almost!", "correct!").

The test for any proposed feature: **does the learner generate, and does the learner alone judge?** Both yes: inside the line. Either no: outside, rejected. The map itself is the answer key, and the learner's own comparison is the feedback channel. A teach-back partner witnesses and reflects what they heard; they do not mark it.

## The daily practice spine

The course has exactly **one** daily practice: a 5–10 minute morning sit that starts as a Beep! Book capture (Phase A, Modules 00–04) and folds a Feelings Form bar reading into the same sit from Module 5 (Phase B). Module 8 adds one line item to the Form, not a practice. One anchor, evolving content, honoring the Experiment Bank's one-at-a-time bandwidth rule.

**[Practice/Daily Practice Spine.md](Practice/Daily%20Practice%20Spine.md) is the binding spec**: the exact sit steps, the script wiring (Scripts 2/3/5/9 at fixed flow points), the canonical header lines every module pastes, and the rule that no module may invent a second daily practice. Module writers amend that file or they do not touch the spine.

## Sittings architecture

Every module runs in **2–3 sittings** (two for Low, three for High and the heavier Mediums). The files stay whole; the sittings are marked, not split. This is the cognitive-load fix: a learner who reads a High module in one push arrives at the embodied practice depleted.

The break block is uniform across all modules. Format (the spec; module writers paste and fill):

```
---

**End of Sitting 1 (of 3).** Stop here. Stand, water, daylight if you can get it.
Come back after a real break — an hour minimum, a day is fine.
Sitting 2 opens with: [name of the next section].

---
```

Three rules: the marker names which sitting just ended and how many there are; it names what the next sitting opens with (so re-entry needs no re-reading); and the module's flow table shows the same sitting boundaries. Practices are never split across a sitting boundary mid-practice.

## Teach-backs and the feedback repeat (why these exist)

Doc 01's v1 edition named the fatal failure mode: **intellectual swallowing**. A learner consumes the distinctions, nods, and installs nothing; procedure without verbal command of the map degrades within weeks, and the High block sits right after the point where v1's practice-quality feedback ended (Module 4).

v2's countermeasures, scheduled, not optional:

- **Teach-back #1, Module 3** · **#2, Module 6** · **#3, Module 9.** The learner teaches one named map to their partner in a voice message, in their own words, before the module's exchange. The partner reflects what they heard land and what stayed fog. Framed as verified-in-lived-experience, never as a test. Source material: the relevant Map Note's "How to explain it verbally" paragraph, read *after* the attempt, not before.
- **The Module 8 feedback-quality repeat.** Module 4's feedback round runs again at Module 8 on the learner's current material. By Module 8 the exchanges have been witnessing-only for four modules; the repeat re-calibrates the practice quality right where the course's hardest relational work happens.

## How distinctions cascade (prerequisite chains, kept from v1)

- *Box vs you* (Module 2) becomes operational only after *new context* (Module 1). Without a new context, "I have a Box" is self-criticism.
- *Liquid state* (Module 3) requires a visible Box to loosen.
- *Feeling vs emotion* (Module 5) requires the five bodies (Module 3), specifically emotional body vs intellectual body.
- *Low drama* (Module 7) requires the new map of feelings (Module 5); drama is structurally an avoidance of clean feeling.
- *Three powers* (Module 10) requires *radical responsibility* (Module 1) and *bright principles* (Module 10). Without those, "I declare" is grandiosity.

The course resists skipping ahead. The **readiness checks** enforce it where it matters: 10-second self-checks before each High module (in the [Learning Self-Assessment](Facilitator%20Resources/Learning%20Self-Assessment.md), Part 2) plus the structural unlock checklist in Doc 04 §D. A learner who fails a readiness self-check re-runs the named prior practice; that is the check working.

## Shipped-format specs (what each artifact actually is)

The single source for *which* artifacts each module has is the [Course Manifest](00%20-%20Course%20Manifest.md) §1 table. This section specs the formats; it replaces v1's Map Note template section, which described a format that no longer shipped.

**Module file** (in `Days/`), in order:

1. Header block: title · purpose · intensity tag · **daily-spine phase line** (verbatim from the Spine spec) · honest time estimate · sittings count · core PM concepts · learning outcomes. High modules add: consent block pointer, GROUND NOW pointer, re-entry link ([Practice/Coming Back](Practice/Coming%20Back.md)).
2. **Free-recall warm-up** (5 min, prior module's central map).
3. Maps introduced (each with embed where shipped, "Study the map" Atlas link, Map Note link).
4. Module flow table with sitting boundaries and "Run the practice" tool links at the moment of use.
5. Concept teaching notes (with "Common misunderstandings" refutation blocks after every map, and "what to expect" calibration plus named drift signals on every micro-practice).
6. Embodied practice (solo) · 7. Partner exchange (async) · 8. Between-module experiment (Day-4 format: *what / by when / what I will notice*, one at a time, plus one callback rep to a prior instrument) · 9. Reflection prompts · 10. Safety callouts (module-specific predicted failure modes; Screen-4 variant cue where flagged) · 11. **Close-the-loop self-check** (three-word scale) · 12. Cohort feed post · 13. Glossary additions · 14. World Copyleft footer.

Solo-reader boxes appear where the Manifest flags them (Modules 00, 5, 6, 7, 9). A videos-are-enrichment note replaces every v1 video reference, pointing to the [Video Manifest](Facilitator%20Resources/Video%20Manifest.md).

**Map Note card** (in `Map Notes/`): map name + one-sentence framing · embedded map image (or text-first note) · **What it is** paragraph · **At a glance** compressed distinctions · the card block linking full teaching (module) and interactive tool (Atlas page). Cards are the distinction-density layer and the teach-back source; they stay terse.

**Atlas page** (in `Map Atlas/`): one map, self-contained HTML on the shared design system; the map walked through interactively; unscored flip-cards framed as recall prompts; GROUND NOW in the header; completion dot + reflections in `etb_atlas_M##` localStorage; links back to module and card. Atlas M12 carries no live EHP: it describes and routes to the gated Day 6 walker.

**Interactive tool** (in `Interactive Tools/Day NN/`): the module's "Run the practice" surface. Conventions: shared design system; per-visit consent component on gated tools (never persisted, day-specific items, `inert` gating, GROUND NOW reachable from the gate); localStorage only, keys registered in the Manifest §6; escapeHtml on all user input; real keyboard a11y; self-hosted fonts, zero external calls.

## Voice

The full voice spec lives in the **[Voice and Style Guide](Facilitator%20Resources/Voice%20and%20Style%20Guide.md)** and is binding for every contributor. In one paragraph: direct, distinction-rich, occasionally blunt, closer to Callahan than to corporate L&D; PM vocabulary unsoftened; every paragraph survives the cut test; concrete verbs; no motivational filler; sources cited where a learner might verify; safety always names a trigger signal, a physical action, and a next decision. The guide adds the enforcement layer: em-dash budget, signature-word caps, banned patterns, the canonical-phrasing ledger, and the verbatim voice-anchor scripts. Run its pre-flight checklist before submitting any file.

## Versioning and revision

This is **course architecture v2**. The locked formats (module shape, sittings block, spine header lines) and the four working agreements cannot change mid-cohort, only between full runs. Teaching content revises continuously as post-cohort reviews (Doc 04 §L) surface patterns. The retrieval line above does not version: it is settled.
