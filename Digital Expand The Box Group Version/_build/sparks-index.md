## Verified entries (page 1 read in full)

### Spark 021
- **Distinction (verbatim):** "Feedback is gold."
- **Gist:** A map is useless without the "YOU ARE HERE" x, and feedback is the only place the x comes from; the world is a giant feedback generator, and feedback is a neutral report of what worked and what did not, not an attack.
- **Useful verbatim line (page 1):** "The world is a giant feedback generator."
- **Experiment (SPARK021.01):** List everyone you have ever been in conflict with; in one week, contact your top ten living "enemies" in person, by phone, or by hand-written letter and ask for direct, honest, confrontive feedback. Do not interrupt, do not explain, do not defend. When they finish: "Thank you." Pause. "Anything else?" Write down what they said; hand-write thanks-for-the-feedback notes.
- **Cited in:** Module 04 (feedback teaching).

### Spark 034
- **Distinction (verbatim):** "There is a difference between positive feedback and praise. Praise is conditioning."
- **Gist:** Positive feedback is neutral and impersonal ("That worked."); praise carries a hidden agenda to control future behavior — dog treats in word form. Same split on the negative side: feedback vs blame. Both praise and blame usurp the listener's responsibility to decide what to do.
- **Experiment (SPARK034.01):** Stop praising, stop blaming. Replace praise with clean wants ("I want you to keep doing that"), replace blame with clean boundaries ("I want you to stop doing that"). Receive both positive and negative feedback with the same sentence: "Thanks for the feedback."
- **Cited in:** Module 04 (feedback vs criticism vs advice teaching).

### Spark 037
- **Distinction (verbatim):** "The best way to learn something is to teach it."
- **Gist:** What you have learned only lives as you give it away; explaining a distinction to another person is itself the learning move, with the warning not to set up a superior teacher-to-student dynamic.
- **Experiment (SPARK037.01):** Assemble a small regular learning group (about eight people) at your house, hold the space, disallow discussions, ask dangerous questions you do not have answers for, start and end on time.
- **Cited in:** Module 06 (teach-back #2 framing). Also fits Module 04's partner exchange.

### Spark 098
- **Distinction (verbatim):** "There are two kinds of learning: defensive and expansive."
- **Gist:** Defensive learning fits new pieces into what you already know and ignores what does not fit (you can only learn about what you already know about); expansive learning lets unrecognizable information exist in you without forcing it to fit, is nonlinear, and feels disorderly — that disorder is what discovery costs.
- **Experiment (SPARK098.01):** Ask three "dangerous" people (people who know your weaknesses, whose Box differs from yours) to write down experiences that would add a new dimension to your development plus three specific ways to learn about them; do not read the papers until later, with a friend; run at least two of the suggestions that week.
- **Cited in:** Module 04 (rapid learning teaching).

### Spark 153
- **Distinction (verbatim):** "There is no learning step during Gremlin feeding."
- **Gist:** When the Gremlin takes over (identity theft: you perceive and act from its reference frame believing it is the only one), it amplifies shreds of evidence into low-drama stories and feeds; no matter how justified the story, no learning happens during the feed. Page 1 also confirms canon: the initiated adult has a conscious Gremlin on a short chain "ready to obey instructions for creating High Drama" — high drama is conscious, not gremlin food.
- **Experiment (SPARK153.01):** Level flight first: split off ~20% of your attention as neutral, non-judgmental eyes looking back at your Box in present time; detect which "I" is speaking and what its purpose is; make no story about it. Neutral observation builds matrix.
- **Cited in:** Module 07 (gremlin / drama teaching; also corroborates the high-drama canon fix).

### Spark 008
- **Distinction (verbatim):** "You create reality with Is-Glue."
- **Gist:** The word "is" does not exist in nature; you use it (creatively, unconsciously) to manipulate how you and other people experience the world, gluing together two things that are not together in reality.
- **Cited in:** Module 10 (is-glue teaching).

### Spark 013
- **Distinction (verbatim):** "You can responsibly choose, declare, and ask best when you are centered."
- **Gist:** Not knowing about your center does not make it unimportant; being centered is the working condition for the three powers.
- **Cited in:** Module 10 (Three Powers teaching; ties the powers back to the Module 3 centering equipment).

### Spark 016
- **Distinction (verbatim):** "The space determines what is possible."
- **Gist:** Space as a PM concept, starting from the physical room analogy: the characteristics of a space establish what can happen inside it.
- **Cited in:** Module 10 (Map of Possibility teaching).

### Spark 041
- **Distinction (verbatim):** "Having a conversation about the conversation creates the possibility of possibility."
- **Gist:** Page 1 names this the meta-conversation, the move that steps out of stuck content into the structure of the exchange.
- **Cited in:** Module 08 (meta-conversation teaching).

### Spark 044
- **Distinction (verbatim):** "Ninety-nine percent of the possibilities available to you right now are invisible to you."
- **Gist:** You are soaking in an endless bath of alternative possibilities; the statement is neither metaphor nor fantasy — the filtering is the Box's doing.
- **Cited in:** Module 10 (Map of Possibility teaching; the Box-as-filter claim).
