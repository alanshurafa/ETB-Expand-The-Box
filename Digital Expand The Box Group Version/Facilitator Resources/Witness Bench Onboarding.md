# Witness Bench Onboarding

| | |
|---|---|
| **For** | The CM and operator: recruiting, preparing, and rotating bench witnesses |
| **What it is** | The onboarding protocol for the witness bench — the small standing list of people who can hold a single witnessed exchange when a learner's partner is unavailable |
| **Reads alongside** | [Async Partner Pairing Playbook](Async%20Partner%20Pairing%20Playbook.md) §6 (when and how the bench is deployed) · `04 - Container and Gatekeeping Protocol.md` §B and §I · `03 - Safety and Facilitation Framework.md` (witness annex) · [Partner Emergency Card](Partner%20Emergency%20Card.md) |

The Pairing Playbook names the witness bench as the first-priority source for a witness-partner substitute and assumes it exists: "a small list, 2–4 people, refreshed each cohort." This file is where that list comes from. Without it, every pairing failure lands on the CM personally, which both overloads the CM and compromises triage neutrality (the CM who just witnessed a learner's EHP debrief is no longer a neutral reader of that learner's feed posts).

---

## 1. What a bench witness does

Exactly one job, in up to three situations:

- **The cadence bridge** (Doc 04 §I). Partners have drifted more than one module apart for 4+ days and a High module is blocked. The bencher holds **one exchange**: receives the learner's voice message for that module's partner prompt, listens all the way through, and replies in the standard three-point shape (what I heard · what I noticed in myself · one question). Then the original pairing resumes.
- **The closing witness** (Module 11). A finishing learner's partner has dropped out or gone silent, and the closing declaration needs a real witness. Same single-exchange shape, with the Module 11 closing prompt.
- **The re-entry witness.** A learner returning from a 30+ day absence re-enters through a witnessed exchange before rejoining the cohort rhythm. One exchange, the re-entry prompt.

One exchange means one exchange. A bencher who is still messaging the learner a week later has left the role; the CM names it and closes it.

## 2. What benchers are NOT

State this in the invitation message, in these words or plainer ones:

- **Not therapists.** A bencher never interprets, diagnoses, treats, or uses clinical vocabulary. If clinical material surfaces, the bencher runs the overflow script (section 5) and contacts the CM the same day.
- **Not coaches.** No advice, no techniques, no "what worked for me," no homework.
- **Not teachers.** The bencher does not explain the module, correct the learner's understanding of a map, or add content.
- **Not the CM.** Benchers hold no triage duty, read no feed, and make no safety decisions beyond the two moves every partner holds: the immediate-danger question, and same-day escalation to the CM.

The role is the witness role from Module 6, bounded to a single rep: be present while the material moves, reflect what was actually heard, hold confidence, and be honest about limits.

## 3. Who qualifies

Minimum bar, all four required:

1. **Has done this work in a held container.** Completed this course in a previous cohort, or completed a live Expand the Box, or equivalent sustained PM practice the CM can personally vouch for. Having read the materials is not enough; the bencher must have *been witnessed* themselves, because a witness who has never sat in the practiced seat fills silence with helping.
2. **Can witness without fixing.** Demonstrated in the screening exchange (section 4, step 3), not asserted.
3. **Signs the partner agreement** (Doc 04 §B), all seven lines, including the immediate-danger protocol. The agreement binds the bencher exactly as it binds a course partner. No signature, no bench.
4. **Is reachable.** Can reply to a voice message within 24 hours during the windows they accept.

Disqualifiers: anyone in a close personal or professional entanglement with a current cohort member (the bencher may be assigned to anyone); anyone the CM would themselves need to monitor; anyone currently in acute crisis of their own.

### The cohort-one bootstrap (no alumni yet)

Cohort one has no graduate pool, so the audit's blunt finding stands: the bench starts empty unless the operator builds it by hand. The bootstrap, in priority order:

1. **The CM's and operator's own PM network.** People who have completed a live ETB or equivalent PM training. This is the strongest cohort-one source.
2. **Trained friends.** A personally trusted adult with no PM history can be brought to the bar for the *cadence-bridge* role only, via the full onboarding in section 4 plus one extra step: they run the Module 6 reading and a practice exchange in which they are witnessed first, then witness. A trained friend is not assigned to a closing-declaration or re-entry exchange in cohort one; those go to PM-experienced benchers or the CM.
3. **The CM, for one exchange only, as last resort.** This is the Playbook's existing floor. The bench exists to make it rare.

Two benchers is the cohort-one minimum to schedule a cohort; three is comfortable. From cohort two onward, recruit graduates at the 90-day alumni touchpoint (the [Cohort Calendar Template](Cohort%20Calendar%20Template.md) marks the moment) and let the bootstrap members rotate off.

## 4. Onboarding sequence (done-when style)

| # | Step | Done when |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Invite.** Send the role description: sections 1 and 2 of this file, the expected load (0–3 exchanges per cohort), and the windows you need covered. | Candidate accepts in writing |
| 2 | **Reading.** Candidate reads, in order: the witness annex in `03 - Safety and Facilitation Framework.md` (the spaceholding teaching and witness scope) · the Module 6 witness material — the role of the witness, the solo-EHP scope table, and the witness-overflow script (reproduced in section 5 below) · the [Partner Emergency Card](Partner%20Emergency%20Card.md) · the partner agreement, Doc 04 §B. | Candidate confirms reading and asks at least one question (a candidate with zero questions has skimmed) |
| 3 | **Screening exchange.** The CM sends the candidate a 5-minute voice message of real (not invented) low-stakes material. The candidate replies in the three-point shape. The CM checks: did they witness, or did they advise, fix, cap the story with their own, or perform empathy? One re-try is fine; coaching the role into someone over multiple tries is a sign they are not bench material this cohort. | CM signs off |
| 4 | **Sign.** Partner agreement signed; contact details, platform handle, and availability windows recorded on the bench list. | Both on file |
| 5 | **Brief.** The bencher gets the deployment message template (Playbook §6), the relevant module's partner prompt arrives only when deployed, and the standing rule: *confidentiality is real; the standard you held in your own container extends here.* | Bencher confirms |

The whole sequence fits in a week per bencher. Run it during Week 0; the first deployment window that matters is Module 5.

## 5. The witness-overflow script (the bencher's one safety move)

If the learner's material exceeds what a single exchange can hold (trauma surfacing, ideation, intent to act on the original event), the bencher says so, in the canonical words from Module 6:

> If your partner names material that exceeds the module's scope (trauma surfacing, ideation, intent to act on the original event in their adult life) — gently say so. *"What you are describing is bigger than this exchange can hold. I am glad you told me. I am also asking you to message the CM today, and if there is a clinician you work with, them too. I am still with you. And I am being honest about the limits of what I can do here."* Then continue witnessing. The honesty is part of the witnessing.

After the exchange, the bencher messages the CM the same day with the flag (no content beyond what triage needs). The immediate-danger protocol on the [Partner Emergency Card](Partner%20Emergency%20Card.md) overrides everything: if the answer to "Are you in immediate danger right now?" is yes, crisis services and the CM come first, course protocol second.

## 6. Bench rotation and care

- **Refresh every cohort.** Re-confirm availability and the agreement at Week 0. Silence is a no.
- **Cap the load.** No bencher takes more than two exchanges per cohort without an explicit opt-in to a third. Three is the hard cap. A bench that one person carries is a single point of failure wearing a team's name.
- **Rotate assignments.** Do not send the same learner to the same bencher twice if another qualified bencher is free; the one-rep boundary is easier to hold without accumulated relationship.
- **Debrief lightly.** After each deployment, the CM asks the bencher two questions: *anything you flagged?* and *anything that was heavier than the role should carry?* The second question is the bencher's own wellbeing check. A bencher carrying residue gets pointed to the dissolution script in [Solo Centering and Grounding Scripts](Solo%20Centering%20and%20Grounding%20Scripts.md).
- **Thank and release.** At cohort close, name the bench's service in the operator's close-out (never naming which learners used it), confirm who is willing to stand again, and retire anyone who is done. Being released well is part of why people say yes again.
