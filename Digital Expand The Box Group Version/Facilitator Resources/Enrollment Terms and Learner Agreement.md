## 1. What you are buying, and what you are not

**The content of this course is free. Forever. For everyone.**

Every module, map, practice script, and tool in Expand the Box (Digital) is licensed CC BY-SA 4.0, consistent with the spirit of World Copyleft. You can read all of it, copy it, share it, and run the solo track without paying anyone anything. That is not a loophole. It is the design. The honest free path is the solo track described in Module 0.

**What you purchase is the held container.** Specifically:

1. **Screening.** A real intake process (the five screens in Doc 04 §A) run by a qualified human, so that everyone in your cohort, including you, has been checked for fit before the work starts.
2. **A community manager (CM).** A named, qualified person (the bar is Doc 04 §J) who monitors the cohort, triages what surfaces, answers you within the response windows in section 4 below, and routes anyone who needs more than this course toward the right support.
3. **Partner pairing.** A matched practice partner before Module 1, the pairing tracker, and re-matching if the pairing does not work.
4. **The cohort feed.** A private, moderated space with other people doing the same work in the same weeks.
5. **The witness bench.** Backup witnesses so that an absent partner does not strand you before a High-intensity module.
6. **The cohort calendar.** Live integration calls, scheduled teach-backs, the pause beats, and the 30/60/90-day alumni check-ins after the course closes.

Read the list again. Nothing in it is content. If you leave the cohort, you keep every page of the course. What ends is the container.

## 2. Who can enroll

**You must be 18 or older.** This course works with emotional and psychological material in an unsupervised, asynchronous format. It is built for adults and the intake form asks you to confirm your age. There is no under-18 version of this course.

You must also complete the intake screening honestly (section 3). Some answers route to a conversation with the CM rather than to automatic enrollment. Routing is not rejection. It is the container doing its job.

---

## 3. Your commitments (what you sign up to do)

By enrolling you agree to the following. These are the same obligations the course documents place on every cohort member; they are gathered here so you can see them in one place before you pay.

1. **Answer the intake honestly.** The five screening questions (suicidality, trauma, addiction, coercive environment, mania/psychosis) exist to keep you and your cohort safe. An intake answered falsely defeats the screening you are paying for, for yourself and for the people paired with you. If your circumstances change mid-course, you tell the CM.
2. **Sign and keep the partner agreement.** Before any exchange, you and your partner both acknowledge the 7-line partner agreement (Doc 04 §B): reply within 24 hours, witness rather than advise, hold confidence, act on the immediate-danger question, tell the CM if you cannot continue, never reuse what your partner shares, and no sexual, financial, or professional relationship with your partner during the course. A learner who will not sign item 4 of that agreement (the immediate-danger protocol) cannot be paired and therefore cannot join a cohort.
3. **Keep feed conduct.** The cohort feed is for short, specific reflections about your own work. No advice to other learners, no long confessionals (those belong in the partner exchange), no other people's stories, no theory debate. The CM redirects drift privately; repeated redirection is a conversation, not a strike system.
4. **Use the safety equipment as instructed.** The grounding script, the pause practices, the consent checks before High-intensity modules, and the unlock checklist are part of the course, not optional extras. The container can only hold someone who is using it.
5. **Pace honestly.** You choose your cadence (Doc 02 §I). You do not skip the pause beats, and you do not unlock High modules by clicking through the checklist untruthfully.

---

## 4. Our commitments (what the operator and CM sign up to do)

1. **Response windows.** Routine messages to the CM are answered within 24 hours. Feed posts are read within 48 hours. Anything the triage rubric flags yellow gets a direct message within 24 hours. Anything flagged red gets an immediate response, with a first reply within 15 minutes of the CM or their named backup seeing it, and crisis-line numbers in that first message (Doc 04 §F–G).
2. **CM coverage.** A qualified CM (Doc 04 §J) holds your cohort for its full span, with a named, briefed backup covering any window when the CM is unreachable. If CM coverage fails and cannot be restored, the cohort pauses and section 6 applies.
3. **Privacy.**
   - **No analytics.** The course pages contain no tracking, no analytics scripts, no third-party calls.
   - **Your practice data stays in your browser.** The interactive tools store everything locally on your device. Nothing you type into a practice tool is transmitted anywhere. You can export or delete it at any time.
   - **The feed is closed.** Visible to your cohort only, never public, posts deletable by their author.
   - **The CM logs metadata only.** The pairing tracker records that exchanges happened, never what was said. Personal shares on live calls are never recorded.
   - **Assessment is anonymous.** The Learning Self-Assessment is collected only as a no-name aggregate ([Learning Self-Assessment](Learning%20Self-Assessment.md)).
   - The full platform privacy minimums are in Doc 04 §H, and every component of the platform stack must meet them before your cohort opens.
4. **Screening is real.** Every learner in your cohort passed the same intake you did. Nobody is waved through.
5. **Honesty about scope.** This course is not therapy, coaching, or psychiatric care, and the operator will never present it as a substitute for any of those. You receive a referral packet for your region at enrollment, before content access opens.

## 5. Refunds (the whole tree, plainly)

"Cohort start" means the day content access opens for your cohort. "Platform costs" means the actual per-seat costs the operator already spent on you (payment processing fees and per-seat platform fees), which the operator states as a fixed amount on the enrollment page before you pay.

| When you withdraw | What you get back |
|---|---|
| **Before cohort start** | Everything. Full refund, no questions, no form. |
| **During week one** (cohort start through the day before Module 5 unlocks for you) | Everything minus platform costs. One short exit message to the CM is requested (not required) so your partner can be re-matched. |
| **After Module 5 unlocks for you** | Prorated: the share of the course still ahead of you, counted by modules not yet unlocked out of twelve, minus platform costs. Example: you leave with 4 of 12 modules still locked, you get one third back, minus platform costs. |
| **Safety-routed exit, at any point** | **Everything. Always. Full refund.** No deductions, no proration, regardless of timing. |

A **safety-routed exit** is any ending that the safety framework initiated rather than you simply changing your mind: an intake screen routed you away from this cohort, a triage escalation paused your access and you and your clinician chose not to resume, or the operator ended your participation under section 6. The course does not keep money it took from someone the container could not hold. There is no scenario in which leaving for safety reasons costs you anything.

In every refund case you keep all course materials. They were free before you paid and they stay free after.

## 6. Termination for safety (routing, not punishment)

The operator can pause or end your participation in the cohort if the safety framework requires it: a red-flag escalation under Doc 04 §G, material that exceeds what this container can hold, or conduct that makes the container unsafe for other learners (for example, repeatedly breaking the partner agreement's confidentiality line).

Three things are always true when this happens:

1. **It is routing, not punishment.** The message you receive will name the next right container for you (clinician, crisis support, a later cohort, the solo track) and your referral packet stands. Pause and exit under this section are framed as care because they are care.
2. **You get a full refund** (section 5, safety-routed exit).
3. **You keep the materials and the license.** Ending the container never ends your access to the work.

A paused learner resumes when the learner and their support person agree it is appropriate (Doc 04 §G step 4). An ended enrollment can re-apply for a future cohort; the re-application path is stated in the exit message.

## 7. The fine print, still in plain language

- **Not therapy.** This course is education and practice. The CM is not your clinician. Nothing here diagnoses or treats any condition.
- **Your data.** The operator holds your intake answers and contact details for the cohort span plus the alumni touchpoint window (90 days after close), then deletes them on request or per the stated retention policy on the enrollment page. Screening answers are never shared with other learners, including your partner.
- **The license.** All course content is CC BY-SA 4.0. Full terms in `LICENSE.md`. This agreement governs the container only; it places no restriction on the content.
- **Changes.** If the operator changes these terms, the version you signed governs your cohort.
- **This is an independent re-presentation of Possibility Management thoughtware, not an official Possibility Management training.** That honesty line is in every footer of this course and it belongs here too.
